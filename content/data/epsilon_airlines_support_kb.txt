SECTION A: FARE CLASSES & TICKET TYPES
================================================================================

Epsilon Airlines offers four primary fare classes, each with distinct service
levels, baggage allowances, change and cancellation policies, and SkyCredits
earning rates. This section describes each fare class in detail, along with
Group Booking provisions and the EpsilonConnect Corporate Account program.
All fares are subject to applicable government taxes and carrier-imposed fees.
For baggage weight and dimension limits beyond those listed here, see
Section B. For Voyager Rewards tier-specific overrides to these policies,
see Section C.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A.1  ESSENTIALS FARE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Essentials Fare is Epsilon Airlines' lowest published fare and is subject
to the most restrictive terms of carriage. Passengers booking an Essentials
Fare should carefully review the Essentials Fare Compact Rules below, as
several standard amenities are excluded from this fare class.

A.1.1  Seat Assignment

Essentials Fare passengers do NOT select a seat at the time of booking. Seats
are assigned automatically at check-in (beginning 24 hours before scheduled
departure). The system assigns middle seats first (seats B and E on Epsilon's
standard 3-3 configuration); window and aisle seats are assigned only after
all middle seats on the aircraft have been filled.

  Exception: Voyager Rewards members at the Navigator tier or above may
  pre-select a Standard seat at booking even when holding an Essentials Fare
  ticket, per Section C.3.2. This override does NOT extend to SkyStretch Seats
  or preferred-position seats (bulkhead, exit row).

  EXAMPLE — Maya:
  Maya, a Navigator tier member, books an Essentials Fare on Flight EA-412
  from Chicago (ORD) to Denver (DEN) at $89. Despite the Essentials Fare
  Compact Rules, Maya may select a Standard aisle or window seat at the time
  of booking at no additional charge. However, if Maya wishes to select a
  SkyStretch Seat, she must pay the applicable SkyStretch surcharge of $45
  (domestic) per the schedule in Section E.2.1.

A.1.2  Carry-On Baggage

Essentials Fare passengers are permitted ONE personal item that must fit
entirely beneath the seat in front of them. Maximum personal item dimensions:
18 x 14 x 8 inches (45 x 36 x 20 cm).

  A full-size carry-on bag (22 x 14 x 9 inches) is NOT included with the
  Essentials Fare. Passengers who bring an overhead-bin-sized bag to the gate
  will be required to check the bag and pay the applicable checked baggage
  fee of $35 plus a $25 gate-handling surcharge (total $60). See Section B.1
  for complete carry-on policies.

  Exception: Voyager Rewards members at the Captain tier or above receive
  complimentary carry-on privileges on all fare classes, including Essentials
  Fare, per Section C.4.1.

A.1.3  Checked Baggage

Essentials Fare does not include any free checked baggage. Checked bag fees
for Essentials Fare passengers are:

    1st checked bag:    $35
    2nd checked bag:    $45
    3rd checked bag:    $150
    Each additional:    $200

  Maximum weight per bag: 50 lbs (23 kg). Overweight bags (51-70 lbs) incur
  an additional $100 surcharge per bag. Bags exceeding 70 lbs (32 kg) are
  not accepted. Maximum linear dimensions: 62 inches (157 cm). Oversized bags
  (63-80 linear inches) incur an additional $200 surcharge. Bags exceeding
  80 linear inches are not accepted as checked luggage and must be shipped
  as cargo. See Section B.2 for the full overweight and oversized schedule.

A.1.4  Changes and Same-Day Modifications

  Advance changes (more than 24 hours before departure):
    Change fee:       $39 per passenger per direction
    Fare difference:  Required. If the new fare is lower, no credit is issued.
    Method:           Online at epsilonairlines.com, via the Epsilon Airlines
                      mobile app, or by calling 1-800-EPSILON.

  Same-day changes:
    Essentials Fare passengers are NOT eligible for same-day confirmed changes.
    Same-day standby is permitted at the airport only, subject to a $75
    same-day standby fee. If no seat is available, the fee is refunded.

  EXAMPLE — Derek:
  Derek holds an Essentials Fare ticket on Flight EA-220 from Atlanta (ATL)
  to Miami (MIA), originally $112. He wishes to change to Flight EA-224,
  departing 3 hours later, which is priced at $148. Derek's total cost to
  change: $39 (change fee) + $36 (fare difference) = $75. If Derek attempted
  to change to an earlier flight priced at $98, his cost would be $39 (change
  fee) with no credit for the $14 fare difference.

A.1.5  Cancellations and Refunds

  Essentials Fare tickets are NON-REFUNDABLE. Upon cancellation, the passenger
  receives a future travel credit equal to the original fare minus a $99
  cancellation processing fee. Future travel credits expire 12 months from
  the date of issue and are non-transferable.

  24-Hour Flexible Booking Rule:
    Per Epsilon Airlines Policy EA-2025-024, any ticket — including Essentials
    Fare — purchased at least 7 days before departure may be cancelled within
    24 hours of booking for a full refund to the original form of payment with
    no fees. This rule applies regardless of fare class.

  EXAMPLE — Priya:
  Priya books an Essentials Fare ticket for $134 on Monday for a flight
  departing the following Saturday (6 days later). Because the departure is
  fewer than 7 days away, the 24-Hour Flexible Booking Rule does NOT apply.
  If Priya cancels on Tuesday, she receives a future travel credit of
  $134 - $99 = $35.

  If instead the flight departed 10 days later, Priya could cancel within 24
  hours for a full $134 refund.

A.1.6  SkyCredits Earning Rate

  Essentials Fare passengers earn SkyCredits at 50% of the base fare
  (excluding taxes and fees). The SkyCredits earning rate may be further
  modified by Voyager Rewards tier multipliers as described in Section C.6.

    Base earning:           50% of base fare in SkyCredits
    Explorer tier bonus:    None (1.0x multiplier)
    Navigator tier bonus:   1.25x multiplier (effective rate: 62.5%)
    Captain tier bonus:     1.50x multiplier (effective rate: 75%)
    Admiral tier bonus:     2.00x multiplier (effective rate: 100%)

A.1.7  Upgrades

  Essentials Fare tickets are NOT eligible for complimentary upgrades,
  UpLift Token upgrades, or SkyCredits upgrade requests. Passengers wishing
  to move to a higher fare class must change their ticket to the desired
  fare class and pay the applicable fare difference plus the $39 change fee.

  Exception: Admiral tier members may use UpLift Tokens to upgrade from
  Essentials Fare to Horizon Fare only (not directly to Summit or Apex),
  per Section E.4.3. This exception requires 1 UpLift Token per segment and
  is subject to availability at the time of request.

A.2  HORIZON FARE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Horizon Fare is Epsilon Airlines' standard economy class product,
offering a balance of value and flexibility. It is the most commonly booked
fare class across the Epsilon Airlines network.

A.2.1  Seat Assignment

Horizon Fare passengers may select a Standard seat at the time of booking at
no additional charge. Standard seats include all non-SkyStretch, non-preferred
seats in the main cabin (typically rows 15-38 on Epsilon's Airbus A320neo
fleet and rows 20-52 on Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner international service).

  Preferred seats (bulkhead rows, forward cabin positions) are available for
  an additional fee of $15-$35 depending on route distance, or complimentary
  for Navigator tier and above (see Section E.1.2).

  SkyStretch Seats are available for an additional fee:
    Domestic and short-haul international (under 2,500 miles):  $45
    Medium-haul international (2,500-5,000 miles):              $65
    Long-haul international (over 5,000 miles):                 $89

A.2.2  Carry-On and Checked Baggage

  Carry-on: ONE full-size carry-on bag (22 x 14 x 9 inches) plus ONE
  personal item (18 x 14 x 8 inches) included at no charge.

  Checked baggage:
    1st checked bag:    FREE (up to 50 lbs / 23 kg)
    2nd checked bag:    $45
    3rd checked bag:    $150
    Each additional:    $200

  Overweight and oversized surcharges apply per Section B.2.

A.2.3  Changes and Same-Day Modifications

  Advance changes (more than 24 hours before departure):
    Voyager Rewards Navigator tier and above:  $0 change fee
    Voyager Rewards Explorer tier:             $49 change fee
    Non-members:                               $49 change fee
    Fare difference:  Required in all cases. If the new fare is lower, the
                      difference is issued as a future travel credit.

  Same-day confirmed changes:
    Horizon Fare passengers may request a same-day confirmed change to a
    different flight on the same route, subject to availability.
    Fee: $0 for Captain tier and above; $75 for all others.
    Fare difference does NOT apply for same-day confirmed changes; however,
    a downgrade credit is not issued if the new flight has a lower fare.

  Same-day standby:
    Permitted at no charge for all Horizon Fare passengers. The passenger
    must request standby status at the airport or via the mobile app no
    earlier than 24 hours before the desired flight's departure.

  EXAMPLE — Jun:
  Jun, a Navigator tier member, holds a Horizon Fare ticket on Flight EA-815
  from San Francisco (SFO) to New York (JFK) at $287. He wants to change to
  Flight EA-811, departing 2 hours earlier, priced at $312. Jun's cost:
  $0 (change fee, waived for Navigator) + $25 (fare difference) = $25.
  If Jun were an Explorer tier member, his cost would be $49 + $25 = $74.

A.2.4  Cancellations and Refunds

  Horizon Fare tickets are refundable subject to a $75 cancellation fee.
  The refund is issued to the original form of payment within 7-10 business
  days.

  Alternatively, the passenger may elect a future travel credit for the full
  fare amount (no cancellation fee deducted). Future travel credits expire
  12 months from the date of issue.

  The 24-Hour Flexible Booking Rule (see A.1.5) also applies to Horizon Fare.

A.2.5  SkyCredits Earning Rate

    Base earning:           100% of base fare in SkyCredits
    Explorer tier bonus:    None (1.0x multiplier)
    Navigator tier bonus:   1.25x multiplier (effective rate: 125%)
    Captain tier bonus:     1.50x multiplier (effective rate: 150%)
    Admiral tier bonus:     2.00x multiplier (effective rate: 200%)

A.2.6  Upgrades

  Horizon Fare passengers are eligible for:
    (a) Complimentary upgrades to Summit Fare (SkyStretch) seating when
        available, for Captain tier and above, beginning 24 hours before
        departure. Upgrade priority is determined by Voyager Rewards tier,
        then by fare price paid, then by Voyager Rewards account seniority.
    (b) UpLift Token upgrades from Horizon to Summit (1 token per segment)
        or Horizon to Apex Class (2 tokens per segment, domestic only;
        3 tokens for international segments over 2,500 miles). UpLift Token
        upgrade requests may be submitted up to 120 hours before departure
        and are confirmed no later than 24 hours before departure.
    (c) SkyCredits upgrade bids via the "UpBid" program (see Section E.5).
        Minimum bid: 5,000 SkyCredits for domestic Summit upgrade; 15,000
        SkyCredits for domestic Apex upgrade; 25,000-60,000 SkyCredits for
        international Apex upgrade depending on route distance.

A.3  SUMMIT FARE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Summit Fare is Epsilon Airlines' premium economy product, providing
enhanced comfort and amenities without the full cost of Apex Class service.
Summit Fare is available on all flights operated by Epsilon Airlines mainline
aircraft with 150 or more seats.

A.3.1  Seat Assignment — SkyStretch Seats

All Summit Fare passengers are seated in the SkyStretch section, which
features:
  - Seat pitch of 36-38 inches (compared to 30-31 inches in Standard)
  - Seat width of 18.5 inches (compared to 17.2 inches in Standard)
  - Adjustable headrest with side wings
  - Dedicated overhead bin space (1 bin per 2 passengers, guaranteed)
  - Power outlet (AC and USB-C) at every seat
  - 13.3-inch personal seatback entertainment screen (on equipped aircraft)

  Seat selection within the SkyStretch cabin is complimentary at time of
  booking for all Summit Fare passengers, including window, aisle, and
  bulkhead positions.

A.3.2  Boarding Priority

  Summit Fare passengers board in Zone 2, after Apex Class (Zone 1), active
  military, and passengers requiring extra assistance. Voyager Rewards
  Captain and Admiral tier members boarding in Zone 1 retain their priority
  regardless of fare class.

A.3.3  Carry-On and Checked Baggage

  Carry-on: ONE full-size carry-on bag plus ONE personal item (same
  dimensions as Horizon Fare).

  Checked baggage:
    1st checked bag:    FREE (up to 50 lbs / 23 kg)
    2nd checked bag:    FREE (up to 50 lbs / 23 kg)
    3rd checked bag:    $150
    Each additional:    $200

A.3.4  Changes and Same-Day Modifications

  Advance changes:
    Change fee:       $0 for all passengers regardless of Voyager Rewards tier
    Fare difference:  Required. If the new fare is lower, the difference is
                      refunded to the original form of payment.

  Same-day confirmed changes:
    Permitted at no charge for all Summit Fare passengers, subject to
    availability in the Summit cabin. If no Summit availability exists, the
    passenger may accept a Horizon Fare seat with a future travel credit for
    the fare difference, or remain on the original flight.

  EXAMPLE — Aaliyah:
  Aaliyah holds a Summit Fare ticket on Flight EA-550 from Seattle (SEA) to
  Los Angeles (LAX), purchased for $389. She arrives at the airport early
  and requests a same-day change to Flight EA-546, departing 90 minutes
  sooner. Flight EA-546 has Summit availability. Aaliyah is confirmed on
  EA-546 at no additional charge, regardless of the current fare price of
  EA-546 (which may be higher or lower than $389).

A.3.5  Cancellations and Refunds

  Summit Fare tickets are FULLY REFUNDABLE. Upon cancellation, the full fare
  is refunded to the original form of payment within 7-10 business days. No
  cancellation fee applies.

  Passengers may also elect a future travel credit with a 10% bonus. For
  example, a $389 Summit Fare cancellation taken as credit yields a $427.90
  future travel credit (valid for 12 months).

A.3.6  SkyCredits Earning Rate

    Base earning:           150% of base fare in SkyCredits
    Explorer tier bonus:    None (1.0x multiplier)
    Navigator tier bonus:   1.25x multiplier (effective rate: 187.5%)
    Captain tier bonus:     1.50x multiplier (effective rate: 225%)
    Admiral tier bonus:     2.00x multiplier (effective rate: 300%)

A.3.7  Complimentary Amenities

  Summit Fare passengers receive:
    - Complimentary premium snack box (choice of Mediterranean, Asian Fusion,
      or Classic American selection) on flights over 2 hours
    - Complimentary non-alcoholic beverages including specialty coffee and
      fresh juice
    - Complimentary alcoholic beverages on flights over 3 hours (beer, wine,
      and select cocktails; top-shelf spirits available for purchase at $8)
    - Noise-canceling headphones (on loan; available on flights over 4 hours)
    - Amenity kit on international flights over 6 hours (eye mask, ear plugs,
      lip balm, hand cream)

A.4  APEX CLASS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Apex Class is Epsilon Airlines' premium cabin, featuring the CloudDeck Suite
on widebody international aircraft and recliner-style seating on domestic and
narrowbody routes. Apex Class represents the highest level of onboard service
offered by Epsilon Airlines.

A.4.1  CloudDeck Suite (International Widebody)

On Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner and Airbus A330-900neo aircraft, Apex Class
features the CloudDeck Suite:
  - Fully lie-flat seat converting to a 78-inch bed
  - Suite door for personal privacy (on 787-9 aircraft delivered after 2023)
  - Seat pitch of 78 inches; seat width of 21 inches in seat mode
  - Direct aisle access from every seat (1-2-1 configuration)
  - 18-inch 4K personal entertainment screen
  - AC power, USB-A, USB-C, and wireless Qi charging
  - Individual reading light and ambient mood lighting
  - Premium bedding set (Frette duvet, memory foam mattress pad, full-size
    pillow)

A.4.2  Domestic Apex Seating

On narrowbody aircraft (Airbus A320neo family, Boeing 737 MAX), Apex Class
features:
  - Recliner seat with 8 inches of additional recline versus Standard
  - Seat pitch of 42 inches; seat width of 21 inches
  - Footrest and adjustable lumbar support
  - 10.1-inch personal entertainment screen
  - Power outlet (AC and USB-C)

A.4.3  Boarding Priority

  Apex Class passengers board in Zone 1 (first to board after pre-boarding).
  This is shared with Voyager Rewards Admiral tier members regardless of
  fare class, and Captain tier members on Apex Class tickets receive Zone 1
  as well.

A.4.4  Carry-On and Checked Baggage

  Carry-on: ONE full-size carry-on bag plus ONE personal item, with priority
  overhead bin access (bins above rows 1-4 are reserved for Apex Class).

  Checked baggage:
    1st checked bag:    FREE (up to 70 lbs / 32 kg)
    2nd checked bag:    FREE (up to 70 lbs / 32 kg)
    3rd checked bag:    FREE (up to 70 lbs / 32 kg)
    4th and beyond:     $200 each (up to 70 lbs / 32 kg)

  Note the increased weight allowance: Apex Class checked bags may weigh up
  to 70 lbs (32 kg) each, compared to 50 lbs (23 kg) for all other fare
  classes. See Section B.3 for Apex Class-specific baggage handling policies
  including priority delivery.

A.4.5  Changes and Same-Day Modifications

  Advance changes:
    Change fee:       $0 — unlimited free changes for all Apex Class tickets
    Fare difference:  If the new fare is higher, the difference is charged.
                      If the new fare is lower, the difference is refunded to
                      the original form of payment.

  Same-day confirmed changes:
    Permitted at no charge. If Apex Class is not available on the desired
    alternate flight, the passenger may:
      (a) Accept a Summit Fare seat with a future travel credit for the fare
          difference, OR
      (b) Fly standby for the next Apex-available flight at no charge.

A.4.6  Cancellations and Refunds

  Apex Class tickets are FULLY REFUNDABLE at any time — including after
  departure for the unused portion of the ticket. Refunds are issued to the
  original form of payment within 5 business days (expedited processing).

  No cancellation fee applies under any circumstances.

  EXAMPLE — Kwame:
  Kwame holds an Apex Class round-trip ticket from New York (JFK) to London
  Heathrow (LHR), purchased for $5,840. After completing the outbound
  segment, Kwame's plans change and he no longer needs the return flight.
  Kwame cancels the return segment and receives a refund equal to 50% of the
  round-trip fare ($2,920), processed within 5 business days. If the return
  segment's one-way value at time of purchase was greater or less than 50%,
  the actual segment value is used (as shown on the original itinerary
  receipt).

A.4.7  SkyCredits Earning Rate

    Base earning:           200% of base fare in SkyCredits
    Explorer tier bonus:    None (1.0x multiplier)
    Navigator tier bonus:   1.25x multiplier (effective rate: 250%)
    Captain tier bonus:     1.50x multiplier (effective rate: 300%)
    Admiral tier bonus:     2.00x multiplier (effective rate: 400%)

A.4.8  Stratosphere Lounge Access

  All Apex Class passengers holding a same-day boarding pass for an Epsilon
  Airlines-operated Apex Class flight receive complimentary access to any
  Stratosphere Lounge worldwide, including:
    - Epsilon Airlines-operated Stratosphere Lounges (23 locations)
    - Partner airline lounges in the CloudAlliance network (48 locations)

  Access begins up to 3 hours before scheduled departure. One guest may
  accompany the Apex Class passenger at no charge. Additional guests may be
  admitted for $65 per person, subject to lounge capacity. Children under 2
  are admitted free and do not count as guests. See Section G for complete
  Stratosphere Lounge policies and location directory.

A.4.9  Complimentary Meals and Premium Beverages

  Domestic flights (under 3.5 hours):
    - Multi-course meal service with choice of 3 entrees
    - Full bar including premium wines, craft cocktails, and top-shelf spirits
    - Warm towel service before meal

  Domestic flights (3.5 hours and over) and short-haul international:
    - Full multi-course meal: appetizer, soup or salad, choice of 4 entrees,
      cheese course, and dessert
    - Curated wine list (6+ selections, rotated quarterly)
    - Mid-flight snack service

  Long-haul international (over 6 hours):
    - Pre-departure beverage service (champagne, juice, or water)
    - Multi-course dinner service (amuse-bouche, appetizer, soup, entree
      choice of 5, cheese plate, dessert, petit fours)
    - Dine-on-demand option available between meal services
    - Full breakfast service before arrival
    - Turndown service with premium bedding

  EXAMPLE — Sofia:
  Sofia, a Captain tier member, books an Apex Class ticket on Flight EA-101
  from Los Angeles (LAX) to Tokyo Narita (NRT), a 12-hour flight, for
  $4,200. Sofia earns 200% x 1.50x = 300% of the base fare in SkyCredits.
  If the base fare (excluding $612 in taxes and fees) is $3,588, Sofia earns
  $3,588 x 300% = 10,764 SkyCredits. She also receives Stratosphere Lounge
  access at LAX (with 1 free guest), Zone 1 boarding, 3 free checked bags at
  up to 70 lbs each, the full international CloudDeck Suite experience, and
  all premium dining services.

A.5  GROUP BOOKINGS (10 OR MORE PASSENGERS)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Epsilon Airlines offers special terms for group travel of 10 or more
passengers traveling on the same itinerary and fare class. Group bookings
are managed through the Epsilon Airlines Group Travel Desk.

A.5.1  Eligible Fare Classes

  Group rates are available on Horizon Fare, Summit Fare, and Apex Class
  only. Essentials Fare is NOT eligible for group pricing due to its already-
  discounted structure.

A.5.2  Group Discount Schedule

    10-19 passengers:     5% discount off published fare
    20-49 passengers:     8% discount off published fare
    50-99 passengers:     10% discount off published fare
    100+ passengers:      Negotiated rate (contact Group Travel Desk)

  Discounts are calculated against the published fare at the time of group
  deposit and are locked for the duration of the deposit hold period (see
  A.5.4). Discounts apply to the base fare only; taxes and carrier-imposed
  fees are not discounted.

A.5.3  Name Changes

  Group bookings allow flexible name changes up to 72 hours before the
  scheduled departure of the first segment:
    - No fee for name changes submitted 72+ hours before departure
    - Name changes within 72 hours of departure: $50 per name change
    - Name changes within 24 hours of departure: NOT permitted; the original
      named passenger must travel, or the seat is forfeited

  Up to 10% of the group size (rounded up) may be removed from the booking
  without penalty if done at least 14 days before departure. Reductions
  beyond 10% are subject to a per-seat cancellation fee of $75 (Horizon),
  $100 (Summit), or $150 (Apex Class).

  EXAMPLE — The Westfield Community Choir:
  The Westfield Community Choir books 24 Horizon Fare seats on Flight EA-630
  from Dallas (DFW) to Nashville (BNA) at a published fare of $198 per
  person. The group discount is 8%, reducing the fare to $182.16 per person.
  The choir director submits a deposit of 10% ($182.16 x 24 x 10% = $437.18)
  to hold the group fare for 14 days. Three weeks before departure, 2 members
  drop out. Since 10% of 24 = 2.4, rounded up to 3, the choir may remove up
  to 3 members without penalty. The 2 removals are processed at no charge.

A.5.4  Deposit and Payment

  To hold a group fare, a non-refundable deposit of 10% of the total group
  fare (base fare x number of passengers) is required. The deposit holds the
  group fare for 14 calendar days.

  Final payment is due no later than 45 days before departure for domestic
  flights and 60 days before departure for international flights. Failure to
  remit final payment by the deadline results in automatic cancellation of
  the group booking, with the deposit forfeited.

  Payment methods: credit card, wire transfer, or corporate check. Personal
  checks are not accepted for group bookings exceeding $5,000 total.

A.5.5  SkyCredits for Group Travel

  Individual passengers within a group booking earn SkyCredits at the
  standard rate for the fare class booked, provided each passenger has a
  Voyager Rewards account number associated with their booking. SkyCredits
  are credited to individual accounts; there is no group SkyCredits pool.
  The group booking organizer does NOT earn SkyCredits on behalf of the
  group unless individually ticketed as a group passenger.

A.6  CORPORATE ACCOUNTS — EPSILONCONNECT PROGRAM
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The EpsilonConnect program provides negotiated corporate rates, dedicated
account management, and enhanced reporting for qualifying businesses. The
program is designed for companies with significant and recurring Epsilon
Airlines travel.

A.6.1  Eligibility Tiers

  EpsilonConnect Standard:
    Minimum qualifying volume:  200 annual booked segments
    Benefits: 3% discount on published Horizon and Summit Fares, quarterly
    travel spend reports, online booking tool access

  EpsilonConnect Plus:
    Minimum qualifying volume:  500 annual booked segments
    Benefits: All Standard benefits, plus a dedicated account manager,
    5% discount on published Horizon and Summit Fares, 3% discount on Apex
    Class, monthly reporting, and priority Group Travel Desk access

  EpsilonConnect Premier:
    Minimum qualifying volume:  1,500 annual booked segments
    Benefits: All Plus benefits, plus negotiated custom rates (typically
    8-15% below published fares), dedicated reservations phone line with
    average hold time under 90 seconds, complimentary Stratosphere Lounge
    day passes (quantity based on volume), and executive travel concierge

A.6.2  Unused Ticket Credits

  A key feature of EpsilonConnect is credit rollover. Unused ticket credits
  from cancelled or changed corporate bookings are pooled in the company's
  EpsilonConnect Credit Bank rather than being tied to individual travelers.

  Credit Bank rules:
    - Credits roll over indefinitely as long as the EpsilonConnect agreement
      is active (minimum 12-month agreement term)
    - Credits may be applied to any future booking by any employee of the
      company registered in the EpsilonConnect portal
    - Credits are valued at face value (no depreciation or expiration while
      the agreement is active)
    - Upon termination of the EpsilonConnect agreement, unused credits must
      be used within 90 days or they are forfeited

  EXAMPLE — Riverton Consulting Group:
  Riverton Consulting Group is an EpsilonConnect Plus member with 620 annual
  segments. An employee, Daniel, cancels a $412 Horizon Fare ticket. Instead
  of issuing a credit tied to Daniel, the $412 is deposited in Riverton's
  Credit Bank. The following week, another employee, Keiko, books a $287
  Horizon Fare flight. The travel manager applies $287 from the Credit Bank,
  leaving a $125 balance for future use by any Riverton employee.

A.6.3  Quarterly and Monthly Reporting

  EpsilonConnect members receive detailed reports including:
    - Total spend by fare class, route, and traveler
    - Average ticket price versus published fare (savings analysis)
    - Top 10 routes by volume and spend
    - SkyCredits earned by individual travelers (summary only; individual
      account details require traveler consent)
    - Unused ticket credit balance and aging
    - On-time performance for the company's most-traveled routes

  Reports are delivered via the EpsilonConnect portal in PDF and CSV formats
  on the 5th business day following the close of each reporting period.

A.6.4  Corporate SkyCredits Earning

  Employees traveling on EpsilonConnect fares earn SkyCredits to their

  booked. The EpsilonConnect discount does NOT reduce the SkyCredits earning

  Nadia's SkyCredits are calculated on the $350 published fare: $350 x 100%
  (Horizon base rate) x 1.25 (Navigator multiplier) = 437.5, rounded to 438
  SkyCredits. Nadia pays nothing additional — the company pays $332.50 —
  but Nadia earns SkyCredits as if the fare were $350.

B.1  CARRY-ON ALLOWANCE

B.1.1  Personal Item
All passengers, regardless of fare class, are permitted one (1) personal item
free of charge. The personal item must not exceed 18 x 14 x 8 inches
(46 x 36 x 20 cm) and must fit entirely beneath the seat in front of the
passenger. Acceptable personal items include purses, briefcases, small
backpacks, and laptop bags. Items that do not conform to these dimensions
will be reclassified as a carry-on bag and subject to applicable fees.

B.1.2  Carry-On Bag
In addition to the personal item, passengers traveling on Horizon Fare,
Summit Fare, or Apex Class tickets are entitled to one (1) carry-on bag
free of charge. The carry-on bag must not exceed 22 x 14 x 9 inches
(56 x 36 x 23 cm), including handles and wheels. Passengers holding
Essentials Fare tickets must pay a fee of $35.00 per carry-on bag, payable
at the time of booking, during online check-in, or at the gate.

    Example: Marco, an Explorer tier member flying on an Essentials Fare from
    Denver to Miami, brings a laptop bag (personal item, free) and a
    roller bag measuring 21 x 13 x 9 inches. Because his ticket is
    Essentials Fare, he is charged $35.00 for the carry-on bag. Had Marco
    booked a Horizon Fare, the carry-on would have been included at no
    additional cost.

B.1.3  Gate-Check Policy
When overhead bin space is exhausted, Epsilon Airlines will gate-check
carry-on bags at no additional charge. Gate-checked bags are tagged and
placed in the aircraft cargo hold, then returned to the passenger at the
jet bridge upon arrival. Gate-checked bags are subject to the same liability
limits as checked baggage (see Section B.7). Passengers who voluntarily
gate-check their carry-on bag prior to boarding may receive a $10.00
SkyCredit bonus deposited to their Voyager Rewards account (see Section C
for Voyager Rewards details). Fragile or valuable items should be removed
from gate-checked bags; Epsilon Airlines assumes no liability for damage to
electronics, eyewear, or other fragile contents in gate-checked luggage.

B.1.4  Carry-On Compliance Enforcement
Gate agents are authorized to measure carry-on bags using the sizer located
at each boarding gate. Bags that do not fit the sizer will be checked and
the applicable fee applied. Passengers who refuse to pay the fee will not
be permitted to board until the matter is resolved.

B.2  CHECKED BAGGAGE ALLOWANCES BY FARE CLASS

B.2.1  Essentials Fare
Passengers holding Essentials Fare tickets receive zero (0) complimentary
checked bags. Fees are as follows:
    First checked bag:    $35.00
    Second checked bag:   $50.00
    Third and each additional checked bag: $75.00 each
All checked bags must not exceed 50 lbs (23 kg) in weight and 62 linear
inches (158 cm) in total dimensions (length + width + height).

B.2.2  Horizon Fare
Passengers holding Horizon Fare tickets receive one (1) complimentary
checked bag. Additional bags are priced as follows:
    Second checked bag:   $50.00
    Third and each additional checked bag: $75.00 each
Weight and dimension limits identical to B.2.1 apply.

B.2.3  Summit Fare
Passengers holding Summit Fare tickets receive two (2) complimentary
checked bags. Additional bags are priced as follows:
    Third checked bag:    $50.00
    Fourth and each additional checked bag: $75.00 each

B.2.4  Apex Class
Passengers holding Apex Class tickets receive three (3) complimentary
checked bags, each permitted up to 70 lbs (32 kg) per bag. Apex Class
passengers receive an increased weight allowance reflecting the premium
nature of the fare. Additional bags beyond the third are charged at $75.00
each and are subject to the standard 50 lb weight limit unless an overweight
surcharge is paid (see Section B.3).

B.2.5  Maximum Dimensions for All Checked Bags
Regardless of fare class, each checked bag must not exceed 62 linear inches
(length + width + height combined). Bags exceeding this threshold are
classified as oversized and are subject to the surcharges described in
Section B.4.

    Example: Priya, a Captain tier member flying on an Essentials Fare,
    receives 2 complimentary checked bags per her tier benefit (see
    Section B.8), overriding the Essentials Fare default of 0 free bags.
    Her third bag would be charged at $75.00, the rate for the third
    checked bag under Essentials Fare pricing. For a complete description
    of fare class inclusions, refer to Section A.

B.2.6  Prepaid Baggage Discount
Passengers who purchase checked baggage allowance at the time of booking or
during online check-in (at least 24 hours prior to departure) receive a
$5.00 discount per bag compared to airport counter or gate pricing. This
discount applies to all fare classes.

B.3  OVERWEIGHT BAGS

B.3.1  Overweight Surcharge Schedule
Bags exceeding the standard weight limit for the applicable fare class incur
additional surcharges as follows:
    51-70 lbs (23-32 kg):    $75.00 surcharge per bag
    71-100 lbs (32-45 kg):   $150.00 surcharge per bag
    Over 100 lbs (45 kg):    NOT ACCEPTED — bag must be shipped via
                              Epsilon Cargo Services or a third-party
                              freight provider.

B.3.2  Weighing Procedures
All checked bags are weighed at the check-in counter. Passengers are
encouraged to weigh bags prior to arrival at the airport. Epsilon Airlines
provides complimentary bag scales at all hub airport check-in areas
(Portland, Charlotte, Minneapolis, Austin).

B.3.3  Admiral Tier Overweight Waiver
Admiral tier members of the Voyager Rewards program receive a waiver of
the overweight surcharge on their first overweight bag per itinerary. This
waiver applies to bags weighing up to 70 lbs. Bags weighing 71-100 lbs
incur the full $150.00 surcharge regardless of loyalty tier. Captain tier
members also receive an overweight waiver on their first overweight bag
(see Section B.8 for full loyalty tier baggage benefits).

    Example: Carlos, an Admiral tier member, checks a 62-lb bag on a
    domestic Horizon Fare itinerary. Normally, this bag would incur a
    $75.00 overweight surcharge (since 62 lbs falls in the 51-70 lb
    bracket). However, Carlos's Admiral tier benefit waives this surcharge
    on his first overweight bag. His second bag, weighing 55 lbs, would
    still incur the $75.00 surcharge.

B.4  OVERSIZED BAGS

B.4.1  Oversized Surcharge Schedule
Bags exceeding 62 linear inches incur the following surcharges, applied in
addition to any standard checked bag fees:
    63-80 linear inches:     $100.00 surcharge
    81-115 linear inches:    $200.00 surcharge
    Over 115 linear inches:  NOT ACCEPTED as checked baggage. Must be
                              shipped via Epsilon Cargo Services. Contact
                              cargo@epsilonairlines.com or call
                              1-800-555-CARGO for arrangements.

B.4.2  Measurement Method
Linear inches are calculated as the sum of the bag's length, width, and
height, including wheels, handles, and any external pockets or protrusions.
Measurements are taken by check-in agents using a calibrated tape measure.

    Example: Tomoko checks a large duffel measuring 34 x 26 x 18 inches
    (total: 78 linear inches). This falls in the 63-80 linear inch bracket,
    incurring a $100.00 oversized surcharge in addition to the standard
    checked bag fee for her fare class.

B.5.1  Sporting Equipment
Sporting equipment is accepted as checked baggage subject to the following
fees (charged in lieu of standard checked bag fees, not in addition to):

    Skis and snowboards:           $45.00 per item (boot bag included free

    Example: Diego, a Navigator tier member, is traveling from Austin to
    San Diego with his surfboard (76 linear inches) and one standard

B.5.2  Musical Instruments
Small instruments (violins, guitars, ukuleles, etc.) that conform to

using the seatbelt and must be in a protective hard-shell case at all times
during the flight. Passengers should contact Epsilon Airlines reservations
at least 48 hours prior to departure to arrange instrument seat purchases.

    Example: Ananya, a concert cellist traveling on a Summit Fare ticket

    seat for her cello at $510.00 (75% of $680.00). The instrument must
    remain in its hard case and be seatbelted for the duration of the
    flight.

B.5.3  Fragile Items
Epsilon Airlines accepts fragile items as checked baggage with the
understanding that liability is limited to $50.00 per pound of the item's
weight. Passengers must declare fragile items at check-in and sign a
Limited Liability Release Form (Form EPS-BAG-07). Fragile items include
but are not limited to: artwork, ceramics, glassware, antiques, and
electronic equipment not in manufacturer packaging. Epsilon Airlines
recommends passengers purchase supplemental baggage insurance through our
partner, TravelGuard Direct, available at epsilonairlines.com/insurance.

B.6.2  In-Cabin Pet Travel
Small dogs and cats may travel in the cabin for a fee of $125.00 one-way.

B.7  LOST, DELAYED, AND DAMAGED BAGGAGE CLAIMS

B.7.1  Reporting Requirements
Passengers must report lost, delayed, or damaged baggage as follows:
    Domestic flights:        Within 4 hours of arrival at the destination
    International flights:   Within 21 days of the date of arrival

Reports must be filed at the Epsilon Airlines Baggage Service Office,
located in the baggage claim area at all Epsilon-served airports, or online
at epsilonairlines.com/bagclaim using Form EPS-BAG-01.

B.7.2  Domestic Liability
For domestic flights within the United States and its territories, Epsilon
Airlines' maximum liability for lost baggage is $3,800.00 per passenger,
per incident, in accordance with U.S. Department of Transportation
regulations. This amount applies to the depreciated value of the bag and
its contents. Epsilon Airlines does not accept liability for jewelry, cash,
electronics, medications, travel documents, or other high-value items
packed in checked baggage.

B.7.3  International Liability
For international flights, liability is governed by the Montreal Convention,
which limits carrier liability to approximately $1,780.00 per passenger
(1,288 Special Drawing Rights, converted at prevailing exchange rates).
This limit applies regardless of the number of bags checked.

B.7.4  Delayed Baggage Reimbursement
When a checked bag is delayed (not delivered to the passenger at the
destination within 60 minutes of the flight's arrival at the gate), the
passenger is entitled to reimbursement for essential items as follows:
    $50.00 per day for essential purchases (toiletries, undergarments,
    basic clothing) up to a maximum of 5 days ($250.00 total).
Receipts must be submitted with the delayed baggage claim form. Essential
items do not include luxury goods, electronics, or non-essential purchases.

    Example: Kenji's bag is delayed for 3 days on a domestic flight from
    Austin to Charlotte. He purchases toiletries ($28.00), a change of
    clothes ($47.00), and socks and undergarments ($19.00) over the three
    days. Kenji submits his receipts totaling $94.00 via
    epsilonairlines.com/bagclaim and receives full reimbursement, as the
    total is under the $50.00/day and $250.00 overall cap.

B.7.5  Damaged Baggage
Epsilon Airlines will repair or replace checked bags damaged during
transport, up to a maximum fair market value of $500.00. Damage must be
reported within the timeframes specified in B.7.1. Cosmetic damage
(scratches, scuffs, minor dents) and damage resulting from overpacking or
inherent fragility of the bag are excluded. Wheels, handles, and zippers
are covered. Passengers must present the damaged bag for inspection at the
Baggage Service Office.

B.7.6  Claim Resolution Timeline
Epsilon Airlines will acknowledge all baggage claims within 72 hours of
submission and will provide a resolution (approval, partial approval, or
denial with explanation) within 30 calendar days for domestic claims and
60 calendar days for international claims.

B.8  LOYALTY TIER BAGGAGE BENEFITS

B.8.1  Overview
Members of the Voyager Rewards program receive enhanced baggage benefits
based on their tier status. Tier baggage benefits apply in addition to
fare class allowances and override fare defaults when the tier benefit is
more generous. For complete tier qualification requirements and other
benefits, see Section C: Voyager Rewards Program.

B.8.2  Explorer Tier
No additional baggage benefits beyond the fare class allowance. Explorer
is the default entry-level tier for all Voyager Rewards members.

B.8.3  Navigator Tier
    - One (1) additional complimentary checked bag per itinerary,
      beyond the fare class allowance.
    - Standard weight limit of 50 lbs per bag applies.

B.8.4  Captain Tier
    - Two (2) additional complimentary checked bags per itinerary,
      beyond the fare class allowance.
    - First overweight bag surcharge waived (for bags up to 70 lbs).
    - Priority bag tags for expedited baggage delivery at destination.

B.8.5  Admiral Tier
    - Three (3) additional complimentary checked bags per itinerary,
      beyond the fare class allowance.
    - All overweight surcharges waived (for bags up to 70 lbs).
    - Priority bag tags affixed to all checked bags.
    - Guaranteed first-off-aircraft bag delivery at hub airports
      (Portland, Charlotte, Minneapolis, Austin).

    Example: Wei, an Admiral tier member, books an Essentials Fare ticket
    from Portland to Charlotte. Although the Essentials Fare includes 0
    free checked bags, Wei's Admiral tier benefit grants him 3 additional
    complimentary checked bags. He checks 3 bags weighing 48 lbs, 55 lbs,
    and 63 lbs. The first bag is within standard weight limits. The second
    and third bags (55 lbs and 63 lbs) would normally incur $75.00
    overweight surcharges each, but Wei's Admiral status waives all
    overweight surcharges for bags up to 70 lbs, saving him $150.00. All
    three bags receive priority bag tags for expedited delivery at
    Charlotte.

B.8.6  SkyCredit Earning on Baggage Fees
Baggage fees paid by Voyager Rewards members earn SkyCredits at the base
rate of 5 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent, regardless of fare class or tier.
This includes checked bag fees, overweight surcharges, oversized surcharges,
sporting equipment fees, and pet travel fees. SkyCredits earned on baggage
fees are posted to the member's account within 72 hours of the flight's
departure. For complete SkyCredit earning rates and rules, see Section C.2.

    Example: Lucia, a Navigator tier member on a Horizon Fare, checks a
    third bag ($75.00) and pays an overweight surcharge ($75.00) for a
    67-lb suitcase. Her total baggage fees are $150.00, earning her 750
    SkyCredits (150 x 5 SC/$1). These SkyCredits are deposited to her
    Voyager Rewards account within 72 hours.

C.1  PROGRAM OVERVIEW

C.1.1  General Description
The Voyager Rewards program is Epsilon Airlines' loyalty program, designed
to reward frequent travelers with SkyCredits, tier status benefits, and
exclusive privileges. Membership is free and open to all individuals aged
18 or older. Members may enroll online at epsilonairlines.com/voyager, via
the Epsilon Airlines mobile application, or at any Epsilon Airlines ticket
counter.

C.1.2  Program Currency
The Voyager Rewards program uses two forms of currency:
    (a) SkyCredits (SC): The primary earning and redemption currency.
        SkyCredits are earned through flights, partner purchases, and
        credit card spending, and may be redeemed for flights, upgrades,
        merchandise, and experiences.
    (b) UpLift Tokens: Upgrade-specific tokens awarded to Captain and
        Admiral tier members. UpLift Tokens are used exclusively for
        cabin class upgrades (see Section C.6).

C.1.3  Program Participation
Upon enrollment, members receive a unique nine-digit Voyager Rewards
identification number. This number must be provided at the time of booking
or check-in to ensure proper SkyCredit accrual. SkyCredits cannot be
retroactively credited for flights taken more than 90 days prior to the
request date. Retroactive credit requests may be submitted at
epsilonairlines.com/voyager/retrocredit.

    Example: Haruto joins the Voyager Rewards program on April 15 and
    immediately receives Explorer tier status (the default entry tier).
    He books a Horizon Fare flight from Minneapolis to Austin for $340.00
    on April 22. Because he enters his Voyager Rewards number at booking,
    he earns SkyCredits automatically upon completing the flight (see
    C.2 for earning rates).

C.2  EARNING SKYCREDITS

C.2.1  Base Earning Rates by Fare Class
SkyCredits are earned on the base fare amount (excluding government taxes,
airport fees, and carrier-imposed surcharges) at the following rates:
    Essentials Fare:     5 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent
    Horizon Fare:        8 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent
    Summit Fare:        12 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent
    Apex Class:         16 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent

For a complete description of fare class inclusions and pricing, refer to
Section A: Fare Classes and Ticket Types.

C.2.2  Admiral Tier Total Ticket Price Earning
Admiral tier members earn SkyCredits on the total ticket price, including
all taxes, fees, and surcharges, rather than the base fare alone. This
benefit applies to all fare classes and represents a significant earning
advantage on routes with high tax components, particularly international
itineraries.

    Example: Beatriz, an Admiral tier member, purchases a Summit Fare
    ticket from Charlotte to London for $1,450.00 total, of which $1,120.00
    is the base fare and $330.00 comprises taxes and fees. A non-Admiral
    member would earn 13,440 SC ($1,120 x 12 SC/$1). Beatriz, however,
    earns 17,400 SC ($1,450 x 12 SC/$1) because her Admiral status
    allows earning on the total ticket price — a bonus of 3,960 SC.

C.2.3  Tier Bonus Multipliers
In addition to the base earning rate, members at Navigator tier and above
receive a bonus multiplier applied to the SkyCredits earned on each flight:
    Explorer:    0% bonus (no multiplier)
    Navigator:  40% bonus
    Captain:    80% bonus
    Admiral:   120% bonus

The bonus multiplier is applied after the base SkyCredit calculation.

    Example: Rashid, a Captain tier member, flies on a Horizon Fare ticket
    with a base fare of $520.00. His base earning is 4,160 SC ($520 x 8
    SC/$1). His Captain tier bonus adds 80%, yielding 3,328 bonus SC. His
    total earning for the flight is 7,488 SC (4,160 + 3,328). Compare this
    to an Explorer member on the same flight, who would earn only 4,160 SC.

C.2.4  Partner Earning
Voyager Rewards members earn SkyCredits on qualifying purchases with
Epsilon Airlines partner brands at the following rates:
    Hotel partners:       3 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent
    Car rental partners:  2 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent
    Dining program:       4 SkyCredits per $1.00 spent

Partner earning requires the member to register their Voyager Rewards
number with each partner program. A current list of partners is maintained
at epsilonairlines.com/voyager/partners. Tier bonus multipliers (C.2.3)
do not apply to partner earnings. SkyCredits from partner transactions
are typically posted within 6-8 weeks of the qualifying purchase.

    Example: Svetlana, a Navigator tier member, stays 4 nights at the
    Meridian Hotel (an Epsilon partner) for $840.00 total. She earns
    2,520 SC ($840 x 3 SC/$1) from the hotel stay. Her Navigator tier
    bonus multiplier does not apply to this partner earning. She also
    rents a car through Apex Car Rental (an Epsilon partner) for $195.00,
    earning an additional 390 SC ($195 x 2 SC/$1).

C.2.5  Earning on Ancillary Purchases
SkyCredits are earned on select ancillary purchases at a flat rate of
5 SC per $1.00 spent, regardless of fare class or tier. Qualifying
ancillary purchases include:
    - Checked baggage fees (see Section B.8.6)
    - Seat selection fees (SkyStretch Seats and preferred seats)
    - In-flight Wi-Fi purchases
    - Onboard food and beverage purchases on flights over 3 hours
Non-qualifying ancillary purchases include change/cancellation fees,
same-day standby fees, and unaccompanied minor service fees.

C.4  TIER QUALIFICATION

C.4.1  Qualification Metrics
Tier status in the Voyager Rewards program is determined by two metrics,
of which a member must meet at least one to qualify:
    (a) Tier Qualifying Flights (TQF): The number of qualifying flight
        segments flown on Epsilon Airlines marketed and operated flights.
        Codeshare flights operated by partner airlines do not count as TQF
        unless specifically noted in the partner agreement.
    (b) Tier Qualifying SkyCredits (TQSC): SkyCredits earned exclusively

    Explorer:     0 TQF  /       0 TQSC  (default upon enrollment)
    Navigator:   25 TQF  or  30,000 TQSC
    Captain:     50 TQF  or  75,000 TQSC
    Admiral:     85 TQF  or 140,000 TQSC

C.4.3  Qualification Year
The Voyager Rewards qualification year runs from March 1 through the last
day of February of the following year (February 28 or February 29 in leap
years). TQF and TQSC accumulators reset to zero at the beginning of each
qualification year.

C.4.4  Status Duration
Members who achieve a tier status during a qualification year retain that
status for the remainder of the qualification year plus the entirety of
the following qualification year. This ensures a minimum of 12 months and
a maximum of approximately 24 months of tier benefits.

    Example: Yuki earns her 25th TQF on September 18, 2026, qualifying
    for Navigator tier. Her Navigator status activates immediately and
    remains valid through the end of the following qualification year
    (February 28, 2028). To retain Navigator status beyond that date,
    she must re-qualify during the March 2027 - February 2028
    qualification year.

C.4.5  Tier Status Match and Challenge
Epsilon Airlines offers a one-time status match for members of competing
airline loyalty programs. Matched members receive a 90-day status challenge
during which they must earn 50% of the TQSC required for the matched tier
to retain status. Status match requests may be submitted at
epsilonairlines.com/voyager/statusmatch with proof of current status in the
competing program.

C.4.6  Lifetime Status
Members who accumulate 2,000,000 lifetime SkyCredits (earned from Epsilon
flights only) are awarded permanent Admiral tier status, which does not
require annual requalification.

C.6  UPLIFT TOKENS

C.6.1  Token Allocation
UpLift Tokens are awarded to qualifying tier members as follows:
    Captain tier:    4 UpLift Tokens per qualification year
    Admiral tier:    8 UpLift Tokens per qualification year

Tokens are deposited into the member's Voyager Rewards account on the date
the member achieves or renews the qualifying tier status.

C.6.2  Token Redemption Values
UpLift Tokens may be redeemed for cabin class upgrades on Epsilon Airlines
operated flights:
    Horizon Fare to Summit Fare (SkyStretch Seats):    1 UpLift Token
    Summit Fare to Apex Class (CloudDeck Suite):       2 UpLift Tokens
    Horizon Fare to Apex Class (CloudDeck Suite):      3 UpLift Tokens

Essentials Fare tickets are not eligible for UpLift Token upgrades.
Passengers on Essentials Fare must first change to Horizon Fare (difference

C.6.3  Token Validity and Restrictions
    - UpLift Tokens are valid for 12 months from the date of issue.
    - UpLift Tokens are non-transferable and may only be used by the
      account holder.
    - UpLift Tokens may not be combined with SkyCredit upgrade redemptions
      on the same itinerary.
    - Upgrade requests using UpLift Tokens are confirmed 72 hours prior
      to departure, subject to availability in the upgrade cabin.
    - A maximum of 3 UpLift Tokens may be redeemed per one-way itinerary.
    - Unused tokens expire at the end of their 12-month validity period
      and are not refundable or extendable.

    Example: Isabelle, an Admiral tier member, receives 8 UpLift Tokens
    on March 1 when her Admiral status renews. She books a Horizon Fare
    round-trip from Austin to London. For the outbound flight, she redeems
    3 UpLift Tokens to upgrade from Horizon directly to Apex Class
    (CloudDeck Suite). For the return flight, she redeems 1 UpLift Token
    to upgrade from Horizon to Summit (SkyStretch Seats). She has used
    4 of her 8 tokens, with 4 remaining, valid through February 28 of the
    following year.

C.6.4  Token Upgrade Priority
When multiple members request UpLift Token upgrades for the same flight,
priority is determined in the following order: (1) Admiral tier over
Captain tier, (2) earlier redemption request date, (3) higher lifetime
SkyCredit balance. In the event of a tie across all three factors, the
member with the lower Voyager Rewards ID number receives priority.

C.8  SKYCREDIT EXPIRATION

C.8.1  Standard Expiration Policy
SkyCredits expire after twenty-four (24) consecutive months of account
inactivity. Account activity is defined as any of the following:
    - Earning SkyCredits from an Epsilon Airlines flight
    - Earning SkyCredits from a partner transaction
    - Earning SkyCredits from Epsilon Voyager Credit Card spending
    - Redeeming SkyCredits for any purpose (flights, upgrades,
      merchandise, or experiences)

Any single qualifying activity resets the 24-month inactivity clock. The
new expiration date becomes 24 months from the date of the most recent
qualifying activity.

C.8.2  Admiral Tier Expiration Waiver
Admiral tier members are exempt from SkyCredit expiration for as long as
they maintain Admiral status. If an Admiral member is downgraded to a lower
tier (due to failure to requalify), the standard 24-month expiration policy
takes effect starting from the date of the tier downgrade. SkyCredits
accumulated during Admiral tenure are not retroactively expired.

    Example: Gabriel maintained Admiral status for 3 consecutive years,
    accumulating 520,000 SkyCredits. During this period, his SkyCredits
    never expired. On March 1, 2027, Gabriel fails to requalify and is
    downgraded to Captain tier. His 520,000 SkyCredits remain in his
    account, but the 24-month expiration clock begins on March 1, 2027.
    If Gabriel takes no qualifying earning or redemption action before
    March 1, 2029, his SkyCredits will expire. However, any activity
    (even a small partner purchase) before that date resets the clock.

C.8.3  Expiration Notifications
Epsilon Airlines sends expiration warning notifications to members via
email and the Epsilon Airlines mobile app at the following intervals:
    - 90 days before SkyCredit expiration
    - 30 days before SkyCredit expiration
    - 7 days before SkyCredit expiration

Members are responsible for maintaining current contact information in
their Voyager Rewards profile to ensure receipt of notifications.

C.8.4  Expired SkyCredits
Once SkyCredits have expired, they cannot be reinstated. Epsilon Airlines
does not offer any mechanism for purchasing, restoring, or extending
expired SkyCredits. Members who believe their SkyCredits were expired in
error may submit a dispute within 30 days of expiration to
voyagerrewards@epsilonairlines.com.

C.8.5  Account Dormancy and Closure
Accounts with no activity for 36 consecutive months (12 months beyond
SkyCredit expiration) are classified as dormant and may be closed. Closed
accounts lose all accumulated TQF and lifetime SkyCredit history. Members
may reactivate a dormant account within 60 days of closure notification
by logging into their account or contacting the Voyager Rewards service
center. After 60 days, the account is permanently closed and the member
must re-enroll as a new Explorer tier member.

D.1  24-HOUR FREE CANCELLATION POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.1.1  Epsilon Airlines provides a full refund on any fare class — Essentials
       Fare, Horizon Fare, Summit Fare, or Apex Class — when the cancellation
       is made within 24 hours of the original booking time, provided the
       departure date is seven (7) or more days from the date of booking.

D.1.2  Refunds under this provision are returned to the original form of
       payment. Credit card refunds are processed within 7 business days;
       debit card refunds within 10 business days. Bookings paid with
       SkyCredits are refunded to the passenger's Voyager Rewards account
       within 24 hours.

D.1.3  The 24-hour cancellation may be initiated through:
       (a) epsilon-air.com under "Manage Booking" > "Cancel Reservation";
       (b) the Epsilon Airlines mobile application;
       (c) calling Epsilon Reservations at 1-800-EPSILON (1-800-377-4566).

D.1.4  This policy does not apply to bookings made fewer than 7 days before
       scheduled departure. Such bookings are governed by the standard
       cancellation rules in Section D.4.

       EXAMPLE: Priya books a Horizon Fare on January 5 for a flight
       departing February 20. She cancels on January 6 at 11:00 AM — 18 hours
       after booking. Because her departure is more than 7 days away, she
       receives a full refund of $412 to her Visa card within 7 business days.

       EXAMPLE: Marcus books an Essentials Fare on March 10 for a flight on
       March 14 — only 4 days out. Even if he cancels within 24 hours, the
       standard Essentials cancellation rules in D.4.1 apply, not the free
       cancellation policy.


D.2  CHANGE FEES BY FARE CLASS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.2.1  Essentials Fare Changes
       (a) Change fee: $99 per passenger, per direction, plus any fare
           difference between the original and new itinerary.
       (b) Changes are not permitted within 24 hours of scheduled departure.
           Passengers must cancel under Section D.4 and rebook.
       (c) If the new fare is lower than the original fare, no credit, refund,
           or residual value is provided for the difference. The $99 fee still
           applies.

       EXAMPLE: Tomás books an Essentials Fare for $189 one-way. He wants to
       change to a flight priced at $230. He pays $99 (change fee) + $41
       (fare difference) = $140 total. If the new flight were priced at $150,
       Tomás still pays the $99 change fee and forfeits the $39 difference.

D.2.2  Horizon Fare Changes
       (a) Change fee: $49 per passenger, per direction, plus any fare
           difference.
       (b) Voyager Rewards members at Navigator tier or above: change fee is
           waived ($0). Fare difference still applies.
       (c) If the new fare is lower, the difference is issued as an Epsilon
           travel credit valid for 12 months (see Section D.8 for credit
           validity rules).

       EXAMPLE: Anika, a Navigator-tier member, books a Horizon Fare for $340
       and wants to change to a $290 flight. Her change fee is $0 (waived for
       Navigator+). She receives a $50 travel credit valid 12 months.

D.2.3  Summit Fare Changes
       (a) Change fee: $0. Passenger pays only the fare difference if the new
           fare is higher.
       (b) If the new fare is lower, the difference is refunded to the
           original form of payment within 10 business days.

D.2.4  Apex Class Changes
       (a) Change fee: $0. Unlimited changes permitted.
       (b) If the new fare is lower, the difference is refunded to the
           original form of payment within 7 business days.
       (c) Apex Class changes may be made up to 1 hour before departure,
           including same-day changes at no charge (see also Section D.3).

D.2.5  All change requests are subject to availability on the new flight.
       Fare differences are calculated based on the fare at the time of the
       change, not at the time of original booking. See Section A for
       complete fare class definitions and rules.


D.3  SAME-DAY CHANGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.3.1  Same-day confirmed changes and same-day standby are available for
       passengers holding Horizon Fare, Summit Fare, or Apex Class tickets.
       Essentials Fare passengers are not eligible for same-day changes.

D.3.2  Same-Day Confirmed Change Fee:
       (a) Captain or Admiral tier: $0.
       (b) All other eligible passengers: $75 per person, per direction.
       (c) Apex Class passengers: $0 regardless of tier status.

D.3.3  Requirements for same-day changes:
       (a) The new flight must depart on the same calendar date.
       (b) Origin and destination airports must remain the same.
       (c) The request must be made at least 1 hour before the original
           flight's departure.
       (d) Subject to seat availability in the same or lower fare cabin.

D.3.4  Same-Day Standby:
       (a) Navigator, Captain, and Admiral tier members may request same-day
           standby at no charge.
       (b) Explorer tier and non-members on Horizon+ fares: $30.
       (c) Standby passengers are cleared in tier order, then by check-in
           time, beginning 30 minutes before departure.

       EXAMPLE: Julian holds a Horizon Fare ticket for the 6:00 PM departure
       and is a Captain-tier member. He arrives early and requests a same-day
       confirmed change to the 2:00 PM flight. His fee is $0. If the 2:00 PM
       is full, he may be placed on standby, also at no charge.


D.4  CANCELLATION & REFUND RULES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.4.1  Essentials Fare Cancellation
       (a) No cash refund is available.
       (b) A travel credit is issued for the ticket value minus a $99
           cancellation fee.
       (c) Travel credits are valid for 12 months from the cancellation date.
       (d) Travel credits may be used by any passenger originally named on
           the booking (see Section D.4.5).

       EXAMPLE: Dara cancels her $220 Essentials Fare. She receives a travel
       credit of $121 ($220 - $99 fee), valid until the same date the
       following year.

D.4.2  Horizon Fare Cancellation
       (a) Refund to original form of payment, minus a $75 cancellation fee.
       (b) Refund processed within 10 business days for credit cards, 20
           business days for debit cards.

       EXAMPLE: Riko cancels his $380 Horizon Fare. He receives a refund of
       $305 ($380 - $75) to his original credit card.

D.4.3  Summit Fare Cancellation
       (a) Full refund to original form of payment with no deductions.
       (b) Refund processed within 7 business days.

D.4.4  Apex Class Cancellation
       (a) Full refund to original form of payment with no deductions.
       (b) Refund processed within 5 business days.
       (c) Any UpLift Tokens used for upgrades associated with the booking
           are returned to the passenger's Voyager Rewards account within
           24 hours (see Section C.6 for UpLift Token policies).

D.4.5  Travel Credit Transferability
       Travel credits issued under Sections D.4.1 or D.2.2 may be applied
       toward a new booking for any passenger who was named on the original
       reservation. Credits are not transferable to passengers who were not
       part of the original booking. Credits cannot be combined with other
       travel credits on a single transaction.


D.5  INVOLUNTARY CHANGES (AIRLINE-INITIATED)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.5.1  Schedule Change Greater Than 60 Minutes
       When Epsilon Airlines modifies a flight schedule by more than 60
       minutes (departure or arrival), the affected passenger is entitled to:
       (a) A full refund to the original form of payment, regardless of fare
           class, processed within 7 business days; OR
       (b) Free rebooking on any available Epsilon Airlines flight within 7
           days of the original travel date, at no fare difference.

D.5.2  Schedule Change Between 30 and 60 Minutes
       (a) Passengers are offered rebooking on the next available Epsilon
           Airlines flight at no additional cost.
       (b) No refund is available under this provision unless the passenger
           also qualifies under Section D.5.1.

D.5.3  Schedule Change Less Than 30 Minutes
       (a) No compensation or rebooking assistance is provided.
       (b) The passenger's itinerary is automatically updated.

D.5.4  Equipment Swap Resulting in Cabin Downgrade
       If an aircraft substitution results in a downgrade (e.g., a passenger
       booked in CloudDeck Suite is re-accommodated in SkyStretch Seats),
       Epsilon Airlines will:
       (a) Refund the fare difference between the original cabin and the
           downgraded cabin, processed within 10 business days.
       (b) Credit 500 SkyCredits to the passenger's Voyager Rewards account
           as a goodwill gesture, posted within 48 hours.

       EXAMPLE: Mei is booked in Apex Class on a flight that undergoes an
       equipment swap. The replacement aircraft has no CloudDeck Suite. Mei
       is moved to a SkyStretch Seat. She receives the fare difference of
       $614 refunded to her card plus 500 SkyCredits in her Voyager Rewards
       account.


D.6  WEATHER & FORCE MAJEURE EVENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.6.1  When Epsilon Airlines cancels or significantly delays a flight due to
       weather, natural disaster, air traffic control directives, government
       orders, civil unrest, or other events beyond the airline's control
       ("Force Majeure Events"), the following provisions apply.

D.6.2  Fee-Free Rebooking
       (a) All passengers, regardless of fare class, may rebook on any
           available Epsilon Airlines flight within 14 calendar days of the
           original travel date at no charge and no fare difference.
       (b) If no suitable flights are available within 14 days, a travel
           credit for the full fare value is issued, valid 12 months.

D.6.3  Travel Credit Option
       Passengers who choose not to rebook receive a travel credit for the
       full ticket value, valid for 12 months from the date of issuance.
       Standard travel credit rules (Section D.8) apply.

D.6.4  Hotel and Meal Vouchers
       (a) Epsilon Airlines does not provide hotel accommodations or meal
           vouchers for disruptions caused by Force Majeure Events.
       (b) Courtesy exception: Captain and Admiral tier members affected by
           a Force Majeure cancellation receive a $50 Epsilon meal voucher
           redeemable at airport restaurants within the Epsilon partner
           network. The voucher is loaded to the passenger's Voyager Rewards
           digital wallet within 2 hours of the cancellation notice.

       EXAMPLE: A severe storm cancels Flight EA-412. All passengers may
       rebook free within 14 days. Kwame, an Admiral-tier member, receives
       a $50 meal voucher automatically. His travel companion, Lena, who is
       an Explorer-tier member, does not receive a voucher but may rebook at
       no charge.


D.7  NAME CHANGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.7.1  Minor Corrections (Typographical Errors)
       (a) Corrections of up to 3 characters in a passenger's first or last
           name are permitted free of charge.
       (b) Must be requested at least 24 hours before scheduled departure.
       (c) Can be processed online under "Manage Booking" or by calling
           1-800-EPSILON.

D.7.2  Full Name Changes
       (a) Complete name changes (e.g., transferring a ticket to a different
           person) are not permitted after booking confirmation.
       (b) Passengers requiring a name change must cancel the existing booking
           under the applicable rules in Section D.4 and create a new booking.

D.7.3  Middle Name and Suffix Changes
       (a) Addition, removal, or correction of a middle name or suffix
           (Jr., Sr., III, etc.) is permitted at any time before departure
           at no charge.
       (b) These changes can be made online, via the mobile app, or by phone.

       EXAMPLE: Gabriella accidentally books her ticket as "Gabriela Santos"
       (missing one "l"). She submits a correction request 3 days before
       departure. Since it is a 1-character correction made more than 24
       hours in advance, it is processed free of charge. Her colleague, who
       booked under the wrong first name entirely, must cancel and rebook.


D.8  TICKET VALIDITY & TRAVEL CREDIT EXPIRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

D.8.1  All Epsilon Airlines tickets are valid for travel commencing within
       12 months from the date of issue. Tickets not used within this period
       are forfeited with no refund, except as noted in D.8.4.

D.8.2  Travel credits issued under Sections D.2.2, D.4.1, D.6.2, or D.6.3
       are valid for 12 months from the date of cancellation or issuance.
       The new booking must be completed (not necessarily traveled) before
       the credit expiration date.

D.8.3  Travel credits are non-refundable and cannot be converted to
       SkyCredits, UpLift Tokens, or cash. They can only be applied toward
       the base fare of a new Epsilon Airlines booking (taxes and fees must
       be paid separately).

D.8.4  Reinstatement of Expired Credits (Admiral Tier Only)
       (a) Admiral-tier Voyager Rewards members may reinstate expired travel
           credits within 30 calendar days of the expiration date.
       (b) A reinstatement fee of $50 applies per credit.
       (c) Reinstated credits are valid for an additional 6 months from the
           reinstatement date.
       (d) Each credit may be reinstated only once.

       EXAMPLE: Chen, an Admiral-tier member, has a $275 travel credit that
       expired on September 15. On October 3 — 18 days after expiration — he
       calls 1-800-EPSILON and pays the $50 reinstatement fee. His credit of
       $275 is reactivated and now expires on April 3 of the following year.
       His friend Sofia, a Captain-tier member, has a credit that expired the
       same day; she is not eligible for reinstatement.

E.1  SEAT CATEGORIES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.1.1  Standard Seats
       (a) Seat pitch: 31-32 inches, width: 17.2-17.8 inches.
       (b) Available on all aircraft types in the main cabin, rows 15-38
           (varies by equipment).
       (c) Included at no additional charge for Horizon Fare, Summit Fare,
           and Apex Class passengers. Essentials Fare passengers are assigned
           a Standard Seat at check-in (see Section E.2.1).

E.1.2  Preferred Seats
       (a) Aisle and window seats in rows 6-14 of the main cabin, offering
           the same pitch as Standard Seats but with priority boarding
           proximity and quicker deplaning.
       (b) Surcharge: $15-$45 per segment, depending on route distance.
           Routes under 1,000 miles: $15. Routes 1,000-2,000 miles: $30.
           Routes over 2,000 miles: $45.
       (c) Complimentary for Captain and Admiral tier members on any fare
           class except Essentials Fare (see Section C for tier benefits).

E.1.3  SkyStretch Seats
       (a) Seat pitch: 34-36 inches, width: 18.0-18.5 inches.
       (b) Located in rows 1-5 of the main cabin on most aircraft, or in a
           dedicated SkyStretch cabin on wide-body equipment.
       (c) Surcharge: $45-$89 per segment depending on route distance.
           Routes under 1,500 miles: $45. Routes 1,500-3,000 miles: $65.
           Routes over 3,000 miles: $89.
       (d) Included at no additional charge for Summit Fare passengers.
       (e) Captain-tier members: 50% discount on SkyStretch surcharge when
           purchasing on Horizon Fare. Admiral-tier members: complimentary
           SkyStretch selection on Horizon Fare.

E.1.4  CloudDeck Suite
       (a) Lie-flat seat with 78-inch bed length, 22-inch width, direct
           aisle access in 1-2-1 configuration.
       (b) Available on wide-body aircraft operating routes over 2,500 miles
           and select high-demand transcontinental routes.
       (c) Included exclusively with Apex Class bookings. Not available for
           direct purchase as a seat selection — must be accessed through
           Apex Class fare or upgrade (see Section E.3).
       (d) Amenities include premium bedding, noise-canceling headphones,
           Stratosphere Lounge access at departure and arrival airports,
           and multi-course dining (see Section A for Apex Class inclusions).


E.2  SEAT SELECTION RULES BY FARE CLASS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.2.1  Essentials Fare
       (a) Seat assignment is made automatically at check-in (24 hours before
           departure). No advance seat selection is included.
       (b) Passengers may purchase a specific seat at any time after booking:
           Standard Seat: $9-$15 depending on route. Preferred Seat: $15-$45.
           SkyStretch Seat: $45-$89.
       (c) Passengers who do not purchase a seat are assigned a Standard Seat
           at check-in. Epsilon Airlines does not guarantee specific seat
           location, row, or proximity to travel companions for Essentials
           Fare automatic assignments.

       EXAMPLE: Naomi books an Essentials Fare for $129. She wants a window
       seat and pays $12 at booking for a Standard window in row 22. Her
       colleague Raj, also on Essentials, does not pay and is assigned seat
       32B (middle) at check-in.

E.2.2  Horizon Fare
       (a) Free Standard Seat selection at the time of booking.
       (b) Passengers may upgrade to Preferred Seats or SkyStretch Seats by
           paying the applicable surcharge (see Sections E.1.2, E.1.3).

E.2.3  Summit Fare
       (a) Free SkyStretch Seat selection at the time of booking.
       (b) Free Preferred Seat selection also available if the passenger
           prefers a rear-cabin aisle or window seat over a SkyStretch Seat.
       (c) Standard Seats are also available at no charge.

E.2.4  Apex Class
       (a) CloudDeck Suite seat is assigned automatically upon booking.
       (b) Passengers may select their specific CloudDeck Suite seat (e.g.,
           window-side pod vs. center pair) at booking or any time thereafter
           via "Manage Booking" at epsilon-air.com.
       (c) On routes operated by narrow-body aircraft without CloudDeck Suite
           configuration, Apex Class passengers are seated in row 1
           SkyStretch Seats with enhanced service.


E.3  UPGRADE METHODS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.3.1  Cash Upgrades
       (a) Horizon Fare to Summit Fare (SkyStretch cabin):
           $89-$159 per segment, depending on route and availability.
           Routes under 1,500 miles: $89. Routes 1,500-3,000 miles: $119.
           Routes over 3,000 miles: $159.
       (b) Any fare to Apex Class (CloudDeck Suite):
           $299-$899 per segment, depending on route and availability.
           Domestic routes: $299-$499. International routes: $499-$899.
       (c) Cash upgrades may be purchased at booking, via "Manage Booking,"
           at airport check-in kiosks, or at the departure gate (subject to
           availability). Gate upgrades may be priced $25-$75 higher than
           advance purchase.

       EXAMPLE: Diego holds a Horizon Fare on a 1,200-mile domestic route.
       He purchases a cash upgrade to Summit for $89 at booking. Three days
       later, he checks availability for a further upgrade to Apex Class and
       pays $349 (domestic rate). His total upgrade cost: $438.

E.3.2  SkyCredits Upgrades
       (a) Horizon Fare to Summit Fare: 8,000-15,000 SkyCredits per segment.
           Routes under 1,500 miles: 8,000 SC. Routes 1,500-3,000 miles:
           12,000 SC. Routes over 3,000 miles: 15,000 SC.
       (b) Any eligible fare to Apex Class: 25,000-50,000 SkyCredits per
           segment. Domestic: 25,000 SC. International short-haul (under
           3,000 miles): 35,000 SC. International long-haul: 50,000 SC.
       (c) SkyCredits upgrade requests may be submitted up to 120 hours
           before departure. Requests are confirmed based on availability
           and the priority order in Section E.4.

       EXAMPLE: Fatima has 40,000 SkyCredits in her Voyager Rewards account.
       She requests an upgrade from Horizon to Apex on an international
       short-haul flight (35,000 SC). Her request is placed on the upgrade
       waitlist and confirmed 48 hours before departure when a CloudDeck
       Suite seat becomes available.

E.3.3  UpLift Token Upgrades
       (a) 1 UpLift Token: Horizon Fare to Summit Fare (one segment).
       (b) 2 UpLift Tokens: Summit Fare to Apex Class (one segment), or
           Horizon Fare to Apex Class (one segment).
       (c) UpLift Tokens are earned through Voyager Rewards tier status
           (see Section C.6): Navigator receives 2 tokens/year, Captain
           receives 4 tokens/year, Admiral receives 8 tokens/year.
       (d) Token upgrade requests may be submitted from 120 hours to 24
           hours before departure. Requests are waitlisted and cleared per
           the priority order in Section E.4.

E.3.4  Complimentary Upgrades (Tier-Based)
       (a) Captain-tier members: confirmed complimentary upgrade from Horizon
           or Summit Fare to the next cabin class, 24 hours before departure,
           based on availability.
       (b) Admiral-tier members: confirmed complimentary upgrade from Horizon
           or Summit Fare to the next cabin class, 72 hours before departure,
           based on availability.
       (c) Complimentary upgrades are subject to the restrictions in
           Section E.5.


E.4  UPGRADE PRIORITY & WAITLIST
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.4.1  When multiple passengers request upgrades on the same flight, Epsilon
       Airlines clears requests in the following priority order:

       Priority 1: Admiral-tier member requesting Apex Class.
       Priority 2: Captain-tier member requesting Apex Class.
       Priority 3: Admiral-tier member requesting Summit Fare.
       Priority 4: Captain-tier member requesting Summit Fare.
       Priority 5: UpLift Token upgrade requests (any tier).
       Priority 6: SkyCredits upgrade requests (any tier).
       Priority 7: Cash upgrade requests (any tier).

E.4.2  Tiebreaking Rules
       When two or more passengers share the same priority level, ties are
       broken in the following order:
       (a) Higher Voyager Rewards tier status (e.g., Admiral over Captain
           within the same priority level).
       (b) Earlier Voyager Rewards enrollment date (longer-tenured member
           takes precedence).
       (c) Earlier check-in time.

E.4.3  Waitlist Clearance
       (a) The upgrade waitlist is processed automatically and continuously
           from the time of request until 30 minutes before scheduled
           departure.
       (b) Final waitlist clearance occurs at the departure gate 30 minutes
           prior to boarding. Any uncleared requests are canceled, and
           SkyCredits or UpLift Tokens are returned to the passenger's
           Voyager Rewards account within 24 hours.

       EXAMPLE: On Flight EA-207, three passengers request Apex upgrades.
       Passenger A is an Admiral member (enrolled 2019) using an UpLift
       Token. Passenger B is a Captain member requesting a complimentary
       upgrade. Passenger C is an Admiral member (enrolled 2022) using
       SkyCredits. Priority order: Passenger B clears first (Priority 2:
       Captain requesting Apex via complimentary), then Passenger A
       (Priority 5: UpLift Token, but Admiral tier), then Passenger C
       (Priority 6: SkyCredits, Admiral tier). Only 2 CloudDeck Suites
       are available, so Passenger C remains on the waitlist until the gate.


E.5  COMPLIMENTARY UPGRADE RULES & RESTRICTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.5.1  Eligible Fare Classes
       Complimentary tier-based upgrades (Section E.3.4) apply only to
       passengers booked on published Horizon Fare or Summit Fare tickets.
       Essentials Fare tickets are not eligible for complimentary upgrades.

E.5.2  Route Restrictions
       (a) Complimentary upgrades are available on domestic routes and
           short-haul international routes under 2,500 miles only.
       (b) Long-haul international routes (2,500 miles and above) require
           UpLift Tokens, SkyCredits, or cash to upgrade.

E.5.3  Codeshare Restrictions
       Complimentary upgrades are not available on flights operated by a
       different airline under an Epsilon Airlines codeshare agreement. The
       flight must be operated by Epsilon Airlines (flight numbers EA-xxx)
       to be eligible.

E.5.4  Voyager Rewards Requirement
       The passenger must have a valid Voyager Rewards membership number
       associated with the booking at the time of the upgrade request. Adding
       a Voyager Rewards number after the upgrade window has closed does not
       retroactively qualify the passenger.

       EXAMPLE: Hiroshi is an Admiral-tier member flying a Horizon Fare on a
       1,800-mile domestic route (EA-553). He is eligible for a complimentary
       upgrade to Summit, confirmed 72 hours before departure. His colleague
       Yuki, also Admiral tier, is on the same route but booked an Essentials
       Fare — she is not eligible for the complimentary upgrade. Yuki may
       still upgrade using UpLift Tokens, SkyCredits, or cash per the
       methods in Section E.3.


E.6  FAMILY SEATING POLICY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

E.6.1  Epsilon Airlines will make reasonable efforts to seat children under
       the age of 14 adjacent to an accompanying adult at no extra charge.
       This policy applies to all fare classes but is guaranteed only on
       Horizon Fare, Summit Fare, and Apex Class bookings.

E.6.2  Procedure
       (a) At booking: when selecting seats on Horizon+ fares, adjacent seats
           are held together when a child under 14 is included in the
           reservation.
       (b) At check-in: if adjacent seating was not secured at booking (e.g.,
           Essentials Fare), the system will attempt automatic reassignment
           to place the child next to the accompanying adult.
       (c) If adjacent seating cannot be assigned by the system, passengers
           should call 1-800-EPSILON for manual assistance. Agents will
           attempt seat swaps and, if necessary, may upgrade the child to
           a Preferred Seat at no charge to achieve adjacency.

E.6.3  Limitations
       (a) On Essentials Fare, Epsilon Airlines will make best efforts but
           cannot guarantee adjacent seating, as seat assignments are made
           at check-in and depend on availability.
       (b) The policy applies to children under 14 traveling with a parent,
           legal guardian, or designated adult on the same booking. It does
           not apply to unaccompanied minors (see Section G for
           unaccompanied minor policies).
       (c) This policy covers standard adjacent seating (side-by-side). It
           does not guarantee specific seat types (window, aisle) or cabin
           class for the accompanying adult.

       EXAMPLE: Leila books Essentials Fare for herself and her 10-year-old
       son, Omar. At check-in, they are assigned seats 28A and 31C — not
       adjacent. Leila calls 1-800-EPSILON, and an agent reassigns them to
       27E and 27F at no charge. Had Leila booked Horizon Fare, she could
       have selected adjacent seats 18A and 18B at booking, with a guarantee
       of adjacency.

F.1  DELAY COMPENSATION

F.1.1  General Policy
Epsilon Airlines provides tiered compensation to passengers affected by
flight delays attributable to airline-controlled causes, including mechanical
issues, crew scheduling, and operational decisions. Compensation is measured
from the originally scheduled departure time as printed on the passenger's
boarding pass or displayed in the Epsilon Airlines booking confirmation.
Weather-related delays, air traffic control (ATC) directives, and security
events are excluded from compensation under F.1, though meal vouchers may
still be issued at station manager discretion.

F.1.2  Delay Tiers and Benefits

  (a) Delay of 1 hour to 1 hour 59 minutes:
      - $12 meal voucher, redeemable at any airport food vendor within the
        departure terminal.
      - Example: Passenger Nora Whitfield is booked on Flight EP-412 from
        Cascadia (CSC) to Meridian Bay (MRB), scheduled to depart at 14:00.
        The flight is delayed until 15:15 due to a maintenance check. Nora
        receives a $12 meal voucher via push notification on the Epsilon app.

  (b) Delay of 2 hours to 3 hours 59 minutes:
      - $25 meal voucher.
      - Stratosphere Lounge access granted to Captain-tier and Admiral-tier
        Voyager Rewards members (subject to lounge capacity; see Section E.9
        for standard lounge access rules).
      - Example: Admiral-tier member Carlos Reyes experiences a 3-hour delay
        on Flight EP-788 at Solstice City (SLC). Carlos receives a $25 meal
        voucher and is admitted to the Stratosphere Lounge for the duration
        of the delay.

  (c) Delay of 4 hours to 5 hours 59 minutes:
      - $50 meal voucher.
      - 1,000 SkyCredits deposited to the passenger's Voyager Rewards account
        within 72 hours.
      - Complimentary Wi-Fi access on the rebooked or delayed flight
        (activation code provided via Epsilon app or at the gate).
      - Example: Navigator-tier member Diane Okafor's Flight EP-230 from
        Meridian Bay to Cascadia is delayed 4 hours and 40 minutes. Diane
        receives the $50 voucher, 1,000 SkyCredits, and a Wi-Fi access code
        for the eventual departure.

  (d) Delay of 6 hours or more:
      - $100 Epsilon travel credit (applied to Voyager Rewards wallet,
        valid for 12 months from date of issue).
      - 2,500 SkyCredits deposited within 72 hours.
      - Hotel accommodation if overnight stay is required (see F.3 for
        hotel voucher amounts).
      - Ground transportation to and from hotel.
      - Example: Passenger Leo Tran is delayed 8 hours on an evening flight
        EP-519 from Cascadia, pushing departure to 03:00 the following day.
        Leo receives the $100 travel credit, 2,500 SkyCredits, a hotel
        voucher, and shuttle transport.

F.1.3  Non-Voyager Rewards Members
Passengers who are not enrolled in Voyager Rewards receive all monetary
compensation (vouchers, travel credits) but SkyCredits are held in escrow
for 90 days. If the passenger enrolls within that window, credits are
deposited retroactively. After 90 days, unclaimed SkyCredits expire.

F.2  FLIGHT CANCELLATION

F.2.1  Passenger Options Upon Cancellation
When Epsilon Airlines cancels a flight for any reason, the affected passenger
is entitled to one of the following:

  (a) Full refund to the original form of payment, processed within 7
      business days (credit card) or 20 business days (cash/check). Refund
      includes all ancillary fees paid (seat selection, baggage, etc.).

  (b) Rebooking on the next available Epsilon Airlines flight at no
      additional cost. Fare differences are waived.

F.2.2  Partner Airline Rebooking
If the next available Epsilon Airlines flight to the passenger's destination
is 6 or more hours from the originally scheduled departure:
  - Epsilon will authorize rebooking on a partner airline at no additional
    cost to the passenger.
  - Partner airline options are presented in order of earliest arrival time.
  - Example: Passenger Mila Andersen's Flight EP-304 from Meridian Bay (MRB)
    to Solstice City (SLC) is cancelled at 09:00. The next Epsilon flight
    departs at 17:30. Epsilon rebooks Mila on partner carrier Orion Air's
    11:15 departure at no charge.

F.2.3  Fare Class Protection on Rebooking
  - Apex Class passengers: automatically rebooked in Apex Class on the
    replacement flight. If Apex is unavailable, passenger is rebooked in
    Summit Fare and receives downgrade compensation per F.7.
  - Summit, Horizon, and Essentials Fare passengers: rebooked in the same
    fare class. If the same fare class is sold out, passengers are upgraded
    to the next available higher fare class at no charge.
  - Example: Captain-tier member James Okorie, holding an Essentials Fare
    ticket, is rebooked after cancellation. Essentials is sold out on the
    replacement flight; James is upgraded to Horizon Fare at no additional
    cost.

F.2.4  Cancellation Notification
Epsilon Airlines will notify passengers of cancellations via: (i) push
notification through the Epsilon app, (ii) SMS to the phone number on file,
and (iii) email to the address on file. Notification is issued as soon as
the cancellation decision is made. If cancellation is known 24+ hours in
advance, the passenger is contacted with rebooking options proactively.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

F.3  HOTEL & MEAL VOUCHERS

F.3.1  Hotel Vouchers for Overnight Delays or Cancellations
When a delay or cancellation requires an overnight stay (defined as a
departure delay past 23:59 local time or a cancellation with no same-day
alternative):

  (a) Standard entitlement (Explorer, Navigator, and non-members):
      Hotel voucher up to $175 per night at an Epsilon-contracted hotel.

  (b) Enhanced entitlement (Captain and Admiral tier):
      Hotel voucher up to $250 per night.

  (c) Ground transportation (shuttle or rideshare) to and from the hotel
      is included for all passengers and arranged by the Epsilon station
      manager.

  (d) If Epsilon-contracted hotels are fully booked, passengers may book
      independently and submit receipts for reimbursement up to the
      applicable voucher limit via epsilonairlines.com/claims within 30
      days.

F.3.2  Meal Vouchers
Meal vouchers are issued based on the meal period affected:
  - Breakfast (05:00–10:59 local): $15
  - Lunch (11:00–16:59 local): $20
  - Dinner (17:00–04:59 local): $25

Multiple meal vouchers may be issued if the delay spans more than one meal
period. Vouchers are redeemable at any food vendor within the departure
terminal and are issued at the gate by Epsilon staff or via push notification
on the Epsilon app.

  Example: Passenger Kenji Watanabe is stranded overnight at Cascadia (CSC)
  after his 19:00 flight is cancelled. Kenji receives a $25 dinner voucher,
  a $175 hotel voucher, shuttle transport, and a $15 breakfast voucher for
  the following morning.

F.4  CONNECTING FLIGHT PROTECTION

F.4.1  Missed Connections Due to Epsilon Delay
If a delay on an Epsilon-operated flight causes a passenger to miss a
connecting flight on the same Epsilon booking (single PNR):
  - Automatic rebooking on the next available Epsilon connection at no
    additional fare.
  - If the missed connection results in an overnight stay, hotel and meal
    compensation per F.3 applies.
  - Checked baggage is automatically rerouted to the new itinerary.

F.4.2  Cross-Reference: Section C (Booking Modifications)
If a passenger voluntarily changes their itinerary after a missed connection
(e.g., choosing a different destination), standard modification rules under
Section C apply, including any applicable fare differences.

F.4.3  Customer-Caused Missed Connections
If the passenger misses a connection due to their own actions (e.g., late
arrival to the departure gate, extended time in duty-free shopping, failure
to comply with boarding announcements):
  - Standard rebooking fees apply per Section D.2.
  - No hotel, meal, or SkyCredits compensation is provided.
  - Example: Passenger Ava Lindqvist arrives at Gate B12 in Meridian Bay
    (MRB) 3 minutes after the boarding door closes for her connecting
    flight, despite a valid 90-minute connection window. Ava is rebooked
    under Section D.2 at the applicable rebooking fee.

F.4.4  Minimum Connection Times
Epsilon Airlines enforces the following minimum connection times for
protected connections: Domestic-to-Domestic: 45 minutes. Domestic-to-
International: 90 minutes. International-to-Domestic: 90 minutes.
International-to-International: 120 minutes. Connections booked below
these minimums are not eligible for F.4 protection.

F.5  TARMAC DELAY RIGHTS

F.5.1  Deplaning
Epsilon Airlines will offer passengers the opportunity to deplane:
  - After 3 hours for domestic flights.
  - After 4 hours for international flights.
Passengers may not be denied deplaning after these time limits except
when the pilot-in-command determines that deplaning would pose a safety
or security risk, or when ATC advises that deplaning would significantly
disrupt airport operations.

F.5.2  Onboard Services During Tarmac Delay
  - Water and snack service begins no later than 2 hours after the aircraft
    door closes (or 2 hours after landing, if the delay occurs upon arrival).
  - Lavatory access must be maintained at all times during a tarmac delay.
  - Adequate climate control (heating or air conditioning) must be
    maintained.

F.5.3  Communication
  - Gate agents or the flight crew will provide updates to passengers every
    30 minutes regarding the status of the delay, the reason for the delay,
    and any anticipated departure or deplaning time.
  - Example: Flight EP-655 pushes back from Gate C9 at Solstice City (SLC)
    at 16:00 but is held on the taxiway. At 18:00, water and pretzels are
    distributed. At 19:00 (3-hour mark), the captain announces that
    passengers may deplane via a mobile stairway if they choose.

F.6  DENIED BOARDING (OVERSOLD FLIGHTS)

F.6.1  Voluntary Denied Boarding
Before involuntarily denying boarding, Epsilon Airlines will solicit
volunteers willing to give up their confirmed seat. Volunteers are offered:
  - Epsilon travel credit starting at $200, negotiable up to a maximum of
    $800 per volunteer, at station manager discretion.
  - Confirmed rebooking on the next available Epsilon flight.
  - Meal voucher per F.3.2.
  - Example: Flight EP-101 from Cascadia to Meridian Bay is oversold by 3
    seats. Gate agent Priya Nair solicits volunteers, offering $350 travel
    credit + confirmed seat on the 16:30 departure + a $20 lunch voucher.
    Three volunteers accept.

F.6.2  Involuntary Denied Boarding Compensation
If insufficient volunteers are obtained, passengers may be involuntarily
denied boarding. Compensation is determined by the length of delay to the
passenger's final destination:

  (a) Arrival within 1 hour of original scheduled arrival:
      No monetary compensation required (rebooking provided).

  (b) Arrival 1 to 2 hours after original scheduled arrival (domestic)
      or 1 to 4 hours (international):
      200% of the one-way fare, minimum $400, maximum $800.

  (c) Arrival 2 to 4 hours after original (domestic) or 4+ hours
      (international):
      400% of the one-way fare, minimum $800, maximum $1,600.

  (d) Arrival 4+ hours after original (domestic), or no alternative
      flight offered:
      400% of the one-way fare, minimum $800, maximum $1,600, plus
      5,000 SkyCredits deposited to the passenger's Voyager Rewards
      account.

Compensation is paid by check or Epsilon travel credit at the passenger's
choice, issued at the airport on the day of travel.

F.6.3  Bumping Priority (Last to Be Involuntarily Denied)
Passengers are protected from involuntary bumping in the following priority
order (last to be bumped first):
  1. Admiral-tier Voyager Rewards members
  2. Captain-tier Voyager Rewards members
  3. Navigator-tier Voyager Rewards members
  4. Explorer-tier Voyager Rewards members
  5. Non-member passengers

Within the same tier, the passenger who paid the highest fare is bumped
last. If fare and tier are identical, the passenger who checked in earliest
is bumped last.

  Example: Flight EP-412 is oversold by 1 seat after volunteers are
  exhausted. Remaining candidates are two Explorer-tier members: Tomás
  Herrera (fare paid: $340, checked in at 06:12) and Simone Bellard (fare
  paid: $280, checked in at 07:45). Simone, having paid the lower fare
  and checked in later, is involuntarily denied boarding and receives
  compensation per F.6.2.

F.7  DOWNGRADE COMPENSATION

F.7.1  Involuntary Downgrades
If Epsilon Airlines involuntarily downgrades a passenger to a lower fare
class (e.g., due to aircraft substitution or operational rebooking), the
following compensation applies in addition to a refund of the fare
difference:

  (a) Apex Class to Summit Fare:
      $200 travel credit + 3,000 SkyCredits.

  (b) Summit Fare to Horizon Fare:
      $100 travel credit + 1,500 SkyCredits.

  (c) Horizon Fare to Essentials Fare:
      $75 travel credit + 1,000 SkyCredits.

F.7.2  Fare Difference Refund
The fare difference is calculated as the difference between the fare paid
and the published fare for the class in which the passenger actually
traveled, based on the fare at the time of original booking. Refund is
processed within 7 business days to the original form of payment.

F.7.3  Named Example
Admiral-tier member Renata Voss holds an Apex Class ticket on Flight EP-900
from Solstice City (SLC) to Cascadia (CSC), valued at $2,400. Due to an
aircraft swap to a smaller plane with no CloudDeck Suite cabin, Renata is
downgraded to Summit Fare. She receives: (i) refund of the fare difference
($2,400 minus the $1,600 Summit fare = $800), (ii) a $200 travel credit,
and (iii) 3,000 SkyCredits.

F.8  CREW-CAUSED DELAYS

F.8.1  Applicability
Delays caused by crew availability — including crew rest requirements,
crew illness, or crew transportation delays — are treated identically
to mechanical delays for the purposes of passenger compensation. All
provisions of F.1 through F.3 apply without modification.

F.8.2  Crew Rest Requirements
Federal crew rest regulations are non-negotiable and cannot be waived by
Epsilon Airlines, station managers, or passengers. Epsilon will not reduce
legally required crew rest periods to minimize delays.

F.8.3  Communication
When a delay is crew-related, gate agents will advise passengers of the
estimated new departure time and the reason for the delay (identified as
"crew scheduling" in passenger communications). Compensation tier per F.1.2
is determined by the total delay from the originally scheduled departure.

  Example: Flight EP-730 from Meridian Bay (MRB) is delayed 3 hours and
  20 minutes because the inbound crew exceeded their duty-time limit and
  required mandatory rest. Passengers receive compensation per F.1.2(b):
  $25 meal voucher. Captain-tier and Admiral-tier Voyager Rewards members
  receive Stratosphere Lounge access for the duration of the delay.

G.1  UNACCOMPANIED MINORS (UM)

G.1.1  Age Requirements and Service Tiers

  (a) Ages 5–7: Unaccompanied Minor service is mandatory. Travel is
      restricted to direct (non-stop) flights only. Fee: $150 each way.
      UM service is not available for children under age 5; a ticketed
      passenger age 16 or older must accompany them.

  (b) Ages 8–11: Unaccompanied Minor service is mandatory. Travel
      permitted on direct flights or single-connection itineraries
      (maximum one stop with aircraft change). Fee: $150 each way.

  (c) Ages 12–14: Unaccompanied Minor service is optional but
      recommended. If elected, fee is $100 each way. If UM service is
      not purchased, the minor travels under standard adult policies.

  (d) Ages 15–17: No Unaccompanied Minor service is required. The
      passenger may book and travel under all standard adult policies.
      UM service is not available for purchase for this age group.

G.1.2  Flight Restrictions for Unaccompanied Minors
  - UM passengers ages 5–7 are not accepted on international flights.
  - UM passengers of any age are not accepted on the last flight of the
    day to their destination (to ensure rebooking options in case of
    irregularity; see Section F.2).
  - UM passengers are not accepted on red-eye flights (flights departing
    between 22:00 and 05:59 local time).

G.1.3  Escort and Handoff Procedures
  - A designated Epsilon Airlines staff member escorts the UM from the
    check-in counter through security (where permitted by airport policy),
    to the departure gate, and onto the aircraft.
  - Upon arrival, the UM is escorted from the aircraft to the arrivals
    area and released only to the designated pickup person.
  - The pickup person must present valid government-issued photo
    identification and must be listed by name on the UM authorization
    form completed at booking.
  - If the designated pickup person does not appear within 60 minutes of
    arrival, Epsilon will contact the emergency contacts listed on the UM
    form. If no contact is reached within an additional 60 minutes, local
    authorities may be notified.

G.1.4  Capacity Limit
A maximum of 8 unaccompanied minors are accepted per flight. Bookings are
processed on a first-come, first-served basis.

  Example: Parent David Osei books UM service for his 6-year-old daughter
  Amara on Flight EP-215, a direct service from Cascadia (CSC) to Meridian
  Bay (MRB). David pays $150 for the one-way UM fee, completes the
  authorization form naming grandmother Beatrice Osei as the pickup person,
  and escorts Amara to the Epsilon check-in counter. An Epsilon staff member
  accompanies Amara through to the aircraft. Upon arrival at MRB, Beatrice
  presents her driver's license at the arrivals desk and is reunited with
  Amara.

G.2  PASSENGERS WITH DISABILITIES

G.2.1  Wheelchair and Mobility Assistance
Wheelchair assistance is provided free of charge on all Epsilon Airlines
flights. Passengers are encouraged to request wheelchair service at least
48 hours in advance via the Epsilon app, by calling Epsilon Reservations
(1-800-EPSILON), or through the Manage Booking portal online. If not
pre-requested, wheelchair assistance will be accommodated at the gate on a
best-effort basis; however, advance notice ensures availability at every
transfer point.

G.2.2  Service Animals
Service animals (dogs trained to perform a specific task for a person with
a disability) are permitted in the cabin at no charge, subject to the
following requirements:
  - The passenger must submit a completed U.S. DOT Service Animal
    Transportation Form.
  - A veterinary health certificate dated within 1 year of the travel date
    must be presented at check-in.
  - The service animal must be harnessed, leashed, or tethered at all times
    and must fit within the passenger's foot space or on the passenger's lap
    (for small animals).
  - Service animals are not subject to cabin pet limits (see G.5).

  Example: Navigator-tier member Hannah Moss travels with her service dog,
  a Labrador Retriever named Birch, on Flight EP-440 from Solstice City
  (SLC) to Cascadia (CSC). Hannah submits her DOT form and vet certificate
  at check-in. Birch rides in the cabin at Hannah's feet at no charge.

G.2.3  Emotional Support Animals
Emotional support animals are not recognized as service animals by Epsilon
Airlines. Passengers wishing to travel with an emotional support animal must
comply with the standard pet policy outlined in G.5, including all applicable
fees, carrier requirements, and cabin limits.

G.2.4  Accessible Seating
  - Bulkhead seats with movable armrests are reserved for passengers with
    disabilities who require additional space or who need to transfer from
    an aisle wheelchair.
  - These seats may not be assigned to other passengers until 24 hours
    before departure unless all passengers with disabilities on the flight
    have been accommodated.
  - An onboard aisle wheelchair is available on all Epsilon aircraft
    configured with 60 or more passenger seats, enabling passengers to
    move between their seat and the lavatory.
  - See Section E (Seating, Upgrades & Cabin Configuration) for additional
    seat assignment policies.

G.3  MEDICAL CLEARANCE

G.3.1  When Medical Clearance Is Required
Epsilon Airlines requires medical clearance from the Epsilon Medical Desk
in the following circumstances:

  (a) Supplemental oxygen is needed during the flight.
      Epsilon provides approved portable oxygen concentrators at a fee of
      $175 per flight segment. Passengers may also bring their own FAA-
      approved portable oxygen concentrator at no charge; a list of approved
      devices is available at epsilonairlines.com/medical.

  (b) Stretcher accommodation is required.
      Stretcher service costs $3,500 per flight segment. The passenger (or
      their medical representative) must also purchase all adjacent seats
      needed to install the stretcher (typically 6–9 seats, depending on
      aircraft type).

  (c) The passenger has had a communicable disease within 14 days of
      departure.

  (d) The passenger has undergone surgery within 10 days of departure.

G.3.2  Submission Requirements
  - The Epsilon Medical Clearance Form (Form EPMC-1) must be completed by
    a licensed physician and submitted to medical@epsilonairlines.com no
    later than 72 hours before the scheduled departure.
  - A signed physician's letter on medical letterhead must accompany the
    form, describing the passenger's condition and confirming fitness to fly.
  - The Epsilon Medical Desk will respond within 24 hours of submission with
    approval, denial, or a request for additional information.

G.3.3  Named Example
Passenger Raymond Cho is recovering from knee surgery performed 8 days
before his planned Flight EP-188 from Meridian Bay (MRB) to Solstice City
(SLC). Because the surgery was within 10 days of departure, Raymond's
orthopedic surgeon completes Form EPMC-1 and provides a letter confirming
Raymond is cleared for a 3-hour flight with normal cabin pressure. Raymond
emails both documents to medical@epsilonairlines.com 4 days before
departure. The Medical Desk approves his travel within 18 hours. Raymond
is advised to request an aisle seat (see Section E) and to walk the cabin
periodically.

G.4  PREGNANT TRAVELERS

G.4.1  Travel Without Restrictions
  - Single pregnancy: Travel is permitted without restriction or
    documentation up to and including 36 weeks of gestation.
  - Multiple pregnancy (twins, triplets, etc.): Travel is permitted
    without restriction or documentation up to and including 32 weeks
    of gestation.

G.4.2  Travel Requiring Medical Clearance
  - Single pregnancy beyond 36 weeks (up to 38 weeks): A medical
    clearance letter from the passenger's physician or midwife is
    required. The letter must be dated within 72 hours of the scheduled
    departure and must state the expected due date and confirm fitness
    to fly.
  - Multiple pregnancy beyond 32 weeks (up to 38 weeks): Same medical
    clearance letter requirements as above.

G.4.3  Travel Not Permitted
  - No travel is permitted on Epsilon Airlines after 38 weeks of
    gestation, regardless of pregnancy type or medical clearance.

G.4.4  Recommendations for Long-Haul Flights
For flights of 5 hours or longer, Epsilon Airlines recommends that
pregnant passengers:
  - Wear graduated compression stockings to reduce the risk of deep vein
    thrombosis (DVT).
  - Request an aisle seat (see Section E for seat selection policies) to
    allow ease of movement.
  - Stay hydrated and walk the cabin at regular intervals.

  Example: Passenger Sofia Engström, 34 weeks pregnant with a single
  pregnancy, books Flight EP-610 from Cascadia (CSC) to Solstice City
  (SLC), a 4-hour domestic flight. No medical clearance is required.
  Sofia selects an aisle seat through the Epsilon app. Two weeks later,
  now at 37 weeks, Sofia wishes to fly home. She obtains a letter from
  her obstetrician dated the day before departure confirming her fitness
  to fly and her due date of April 12. She presents the letter at check-in
  and is cleared to board.

G.5  PET TRAVEL

G.5.1  In-Cabin Pet Travel
  - Eligible animals: Small dogs and cats only.
  - Carrier dimensions: Maximum 17 inches long x 11 inches wide x 7.5
    inches high (must fit under the seat in front of the passenger).
  - Combined weight: Pet plus carrier must not exceed 20 lbs.
  - Fee: $125 one-way per pet.
  - Cabin limit: Maximum 2 pets permitted in the passenger cabin per
    flight, allocated on a first-come, first-served basis at time of
    booking.
  - Cabin restriction: In-cabin pets are not permitted in the Apex Class
    cabin. Passengers traveling in Apex Class who wish to bring a pet
    must arrange cargo transport (see G.5.2) or have the pet travel in
    the main cabin under the care of a companion traveling in Horizon or
    Essentials Fare.
  - Flight duration restriction: In-cabin pets are not permitted on
    flights exceeding 8 hours in scheduled duration.
  - The pet must remain inside the carrier with the carrier zipped or
    latched for the entire duration of the flight, including taxi,
    takeoff, and landing.
  - See Section B.6 for pet-related baggage fees and documentation
    requirements at check-in.

  Example: Explorer-tier member Paula Nkosi wishes to bring her 9-lb
  cat, Mochi, in-cabin on Flight EP-320 from Meridian Bay (MRB) to
  Cascadia (CSC), a 3-hour flight. Paula books the pet add-on for $125
  and confirms Mochi's carrier measures 16x10x7 inches. Paula is
  traveling in Horizon Fare, so in-cabin travel is permitted. At the
  gate, the agent verifies the carrier fits under the seat and Mochi
  remains inside throughout the flight.

G.5.2  Cargo Pet Travel
  - Eligible animals: Dogs and cats.
  - Minimum age: 12 weeks old at the time of travel.
  - Carrier: Must be IATA Live Animals Regulations (LAR) compliant —
    rigid-walled, ventilated on at least three sides, with absorbent
    liner and attached water dish.
  - Fees: $250 one-way domestic; $400 one-way international.
  - Temperature restrictions: Epsilon Airlines will not transport animals
    in cargo if the actual or forecasted tarmac temperature at the origin,
    destination, or any connection point is below 20 degrees Fahrenheit
    (-7 degrees Celsius) or above 85 degrees Fahrenheit (29 degrees
    Celsius) at the time of departure or arrival.
  - Brachycephalic (snub-nosed) breed restriction: The following breeds
    (and mixes thereof) are not accepted for cargo transport due to
    elevated risk of respiratory distress: English Bulldog, French
    Bulldog, Pug, Boston Terrier, Pekingese, Shih Tzu, Boxer (dogs);
    Persian, Himalayan, Exotic Shorthair, Burmese (cats). This list is
    not exhaustive; Epsilon reserves the right to decline cargo transport
    for any brachycephalic breed.
  - Cargo pet bookings must be made at least 48 hours before departure
    by calling Epsilon Reservations (1-800-EPSILON). Online cargo pet
    booking is not available.

  Example: Passenger Jordan Kessler needs to transport his 3-year-old
  Golden Retriever, Maple, from Solstice City (SLC) to Meridian Bay
  (MRB). Jordan calls Epsilon Reservations 5 days in advance, pays the
  $250 domestic cargo fee, and confirms his IATA-compliant crate
  dimensions. On the day of travel, the forecasted tarmac temperature
  at SLC is 72 degrees Fahrenheit and at MRB is 68 degrees Fahrenheit —
  both within the acceptable range. Maple travels safely in the climate-
  controlled cargo hold.

G.5.3  Service Animals
Service animals are governed by G.2.2 and are exempt from all pet fees,
carrier requirements, and cabin limits. Service animals are not considered
pets under this section.

G.6  PREGNANT AND NURSING MOTHERS — BOARDING & SEATING PRIORITY

G.6.1  Priority Boarding
Pregnant passengers (at any stage of pregnancy) and passengers traveling
with a nursing infant are entitled to priority boarding immediately after
Zone 1 boarding, regardless of their assigned boarding zone or fare class.
This benefit is extended automatically when the passenger identifies
themselves to the gate agent; no advance registration or documentation
is required.

G.6.2  Complimentary Adjacent Seat Selection
When an adjacent empty seat is available at the time of check-in (beginning
24 hours before departure), Epsilon Airlines will assign it to a pregnant
or nursing passenger at no charge, provided:
  - The passenger has self-identified as pregnant or nursing through the
    Epsilon app, at check-in, or by contacting Epsilon Reservations.
  - The adjacent seat is not already assigned to another passenger.
  - The adjacent seat is in the same fare class cabin as the passenger's
    original booking.

This benefit is subject to availability and is not guaranteed. It does not
apply if the flight is fully booked at the time of check-in.

  Example: Nursing mother Ingrid Holm, traveling on a Horizon Fare ticket
  on Flight EP-505 from Cascadia (CSC) to Solstice City (SLC), checks in
  via the Epsilon app 20 hours before departure and indicates she is
  traveling with a nursing infant. The system identifies seat 14B (adjacent
  to Ingrid's assigned 14A) as unoccupied and automatically blocks it for
  Ingrid at no charge. At the gate, Ingrid boards after Zone 1.

G.6.3  Cross-Reference
For general seat selection policies, including SkyStretch Seats and
CloudDeck Suite assignment rules, see Section E. For baggage policies
related to infant travel (strollers, car seats, diaper bags), see
Section B.
